# Entertainment Press Materials vs. Independent Reporting

## The Spectrum: From Pure PR to Independent Journalism

### Tier 1: Pure PR / Studio-Produced Content
**What it is**: Directly produced by studio/agency in their own voice. Press release, promotional video, branded content, advertorial.

**Characteristics**:
- Explicit promotional intent
- Voice is the studio (not journalist)
- Approved by PR department / legal
- Often contains superlatives ("unprecedented", "breathtaking", "must-see")

**Ethical use**:
- Clearly label as "Press Release" or "Sponsored Content"
- Do not republish verbatim as journalism
- If using facts from the release (e.g., budget, cast), verify independently or disclose: "according to studio materials"

**Example of violation**: 
```
❌ "The film's breathtaking cinematography captures the intimate landscape 
of memory, creating a visual poem of loss and longing."

(This language is verbatim from the studio press kit, presented as 
independent review.)
```

---

### Tier 2: Embargoed Press Materials (but Not Pure Advertising)
**What it is**: Studio provides advance access to materials (review copy, interview transcript, images) with a publication embargo.

**Characteristics**:
- Journalist gets early access (advantage: can plan coverage timing)
- Embargo creates obligation: cannot publish before date/time
- Material is still curated by studio (they decide what to release)
- Can include interviews, production notes, images, film itself

**Ethical use**:
- Treat embargo as a contract; breaking it is a professional violation
- Use the embargoed material as foundation, but supplement with:
  - Independent viewing / observation
  - Third-party sources (critics, analysts, ticket sales)
  - Direct reporting (your own interviews, if possible)
- Disclose embargo origin only if it's relevant to the piece

**Example of proper practice**:
```
✅ "At a studio-provided advance screening, I observed that the film relies 
heavily on long takes and minimal dialogue — a stylistic choice that either 
deepens the emotional core or (as some may argue) tests viewer patience."

The studio supplied the advance screening; I supplied the independent 
observation and the balanced evaluation.
```

---

### Tier 3: Press Junket / Hosted Interview
**What it is**: Studio organizes travel, accommodation, interview opportunity for journalists to conduct interviews with cast/crew on a pre-determined date/location.

**Characteristics**:
- Journalist conducts the interview (not studio)
- Studio controls: location, duration, other attendees, logistics
- Journalist's questions are typically approved in advance or screened
- Audio/video may be "shared" but controlled
- Interview is "on the record" but publicist may be present

**Ethical implications**:
- Free travel + accommodation creates implicit debt to cover the film
- Publicist presence may chill candid responses
- "Approved questions" limit independence
- Shared footage may have embedded restrictions (e.g., "for print only, not broadcast")

**Disclosure standard**:
- Disclose junket origin if there are conditions (approval rights, footage restrictions)
- Example: 「本次受訪係在片商安排的宣傳會上進行;演員經紀公司保留事後編輯權」("This interview was conducted at a studio-hosted junket; the talent agency reserves right of editorial review.")
- Junket attendance alone (if no conditions) does not require disclosure but transparency is better

**Red line**: If the publicist demands approval rights over your quotes, reject the condition or disclose it prominently.

---

### Tier 4: Independent Interview (No Studio Mediation)
**What it is**: You contact talent directly (via their agent, but without studio coordination) and conduct an interview.

**Characteristics**:
- Journalist negotiates terms directly with talent's representative
- No studio approval or oversight
- Questions can be ad-libbed; no pre-approval
- Transcript is yours to use as you see fit (subject to talent's typical restrictions: off-the-record portions, embargo if any)

**Ethical use**:
- Treat as off-record any portion the talent requests
- Disclose any material conditions (e.g., "interview subject requested anonymity on certain topics")
- Otherwise, no special disclosure needed

**Example**:
```
✅ "When I asked director 王大明 about his influences, he cited Koreeda 
and mentioned that the 20-year gap in the mother character's absence 
reflected his own family experience."

(Independent interview; no studio mediation; no disclosure needed 
unless there were special conditions.)
```

---

### Tier 5: Public Statement / Social Media
**What it is**: Talent or director posts directly to Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, or gives public statement to media (not in a studio-controlled junket).

**Characteristics**:
- Talent speaks in their own voice
- Public-facing (everyone can see it)
- May be promotional but not curated by studio

**Ethical use**:
- You can quote or paraphrase, as with any public statement
- Disclose source: "on Instagram", "in a tweet", etc.
- Be alert to context: is this an ad campaign or genuine personal thought?

**Example**:
```
✅ "On Instagram, actress 林小華 wrote: 'Playing this character taught me 
what it means to hold grief.' The post has over 100,000 likes."

(Public statement; straightforward attribution.)
```

## Disclosure Decision Tree

**For every major fact in your piece, ask:**

1. **Where did this information originate?**
   - Studio press release / press materials (Tier 1-2) → Disclose source or verify independently
   - Junket-conducted interview (Tier 3) → Disclose if conditions attached; otherwise optional
   - Independent interview (Tier 4) → No disclosure needed (understood to be your own reporting)
   - Public statement (Tier 5) → Attribute to platform (Instagram, etc.)

2. **Is there a condition I should disclose?**
   - Approval rights by talent/publicist? → **Disclose**
   - Paid/subsidized travel to junket? → Disclose if studio has editorial interest
   - Exclusive interview agreement? → Disclose
   - Off-the-record portions? → Disclose which topics are off-the-record
   - Embargo? → Disclose if necessary for reader context (e.g., "this review is embargoed until film opens"; **must respect embargo timing**)

3. **Is this fact independently verifiable?**
   - Budget figure: check with National Film Center or cross-source
   - Release date: verify with official distributor
   - Casting: verify against IMDb or official studio announcement
   - Quote: use verbatim if from transcript; paraphrase only if you're confident in accuracy

## Common Violations: How to Avoid Them

### Violation 1: "Rephrased Press Kit as Independent Review"
**What it looks like**:
```
Press Kit: "The film's breathtaking cinematography captures a meditation 
on memory, creating visual poetry."

Your piece: "The visual poetry of cinematography explores memory in 
breathtaking detail."

(Same framing, slightly reworked words = still PR capture)
```

**How to avoid it**:
- Read the press kit, then set it aside.
- Write your review based only on what you observed in the film.
- If you use a fact from the press kit (e.g., "filmed in 140 locations"), disclose it: "per production notes".
- Test: would you write this sentence if the press kit didn't exist? If not, the press kit is driving your language.

### Violation 2: "Studio Framing Without Attribution"
**What it looks like**:
```
Studio framing: "This is a blockbuster summer adventure."
Your piece: "This summer's biggest adventure awaits audiences..."

(You've absorbed the framing without attribution.)
```

**How to avoid it**:
- Use concrete details: "Over 140 locations, 2-hour runtime, ensemble cast of X" — readers judge for themselves.
- Avoid loaded adjectives ("blockbuster", "must-see") unless they're specific critical consensus, not marketing framing.

### Violation 3: "Junket Interview Treated as Independent Reporting"
**What it looks like**:
```
You conduct a junket interview where the publicist screened questions 
in advance and the talent had approval rights over the final quotes. 
You then present the quotes without noting these conditions.

Reader assumes this is candid journalism; it's actually a controlled 
media appearance.
```

**How to avoid it**:
- Disclose conditions if they affect credibility: "The interview was 
conducted at a studio-hosted junket, and the talent's representative 
reviewed the final quotes."
- Or: conduct a separate, independent interview (without studio mediation) 
and lead with those quotes, reserving junket quotes for secondary support.

### Violation 4: "Streamed Platform Data as Cross-Platform Comparison"
**What it looks like**:
```
"Demo Night is the #1 most-watched film on Netflix globally; it has 
surpassed the top streaming platforms with 85M watch hours."

(Mixing Netflix's "watch hours" metric with undefined "most-watched" 
across all platforms — impossible comparison.)
```

**How to avoid it**:
- Be specific: "Netflix reported Demo Night has 85M watch hours in Taiwan 
in its first week" (platform-specific, metric-specific, territory-specific).
- Never compare Netflix watch hours to Disney+ accounts engaged without 
explaining the metric difference.

### Violation 5: "Omitted Embargo Date"
**What it looks like**:
```
Material arrives embargoed until 2026-05-02 14:00. You write the review 
on May 1 and schedule it to publish May 2 at 9:00 AM, before embargo lifts.

(You've violated the contract and will be blacklisted.)
```

**How to avoid it**:
- Log embargo dates in a spreadsheet.
- Schedule publication AFTER embargo lift (not before).
- If you accidentally publish early, contact the studio within 1 hour to explain and apologize.

## Transparency Best Practices

### Disclosure Box Template
```markdown
---

**Disclosure & Notes:**
- This review is based on an advance screening provided by Demo Pictures.
- The director, 王大明, was interviewed independently (not via junket).
- Box office and Rotten Tomatoes data are from public sources 
  (National Film Center, RT website).
- Photo credit: © Demo Pictures.
```

### Junket Disclosure Template
```markdown
**Transparency note**: This interview was conducted at a studio-hosted 
press junket in Taipei on April 28, 2026. The studio provided 
transportation and accommodation. The talent's representative reviewed 
final quotes for accuracy.
```

### Embargo Compliance Note (internal, for scheduling)
```
Article: Demo Night Review
Embargo: 2026-05-02 14:00 Taiwan Time
Scheduled Publish: 2026-05-02 15:00 (1 hour after embargo lift)
Verification: ✅ Confirmed with studio PR
```
