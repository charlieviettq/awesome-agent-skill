# Entman's Cascading Activation Model

## Overview

Entman (2003, 2004) extended framing theory to explain how frames flow through political communication systems. The cascading activation model describes frame propagation across five levels, with influence flowing mostly DOWNWARD but with feedback loops.

## The Five Levels

```
┌─────────────────────┐
│  1. ADMINISTRATION  │  ← Top-level frame sponsors
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ ↓↑
┌──────────▼──────────┐
│  2. OTHER ELITES    │  ← Opposition, experts, former officials
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ ↓↑
┌──────────▼──────────┐
│  3. MEDIA           │  ← Journalists, editors, outlets
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ ↓↑
┌──────────▼──────────┐
│  4. NEWS FRAMES     │  ← Published/broadcast content
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ ↓↑
┌──────────▼──────────┐
│  5. PUBLIC          │  ← Audience opinion and perception
└─────────────────────┘
```

## Key Propositions

1. **Asymmetric flow**: Influence flows more easily downward than upward. Administration frames reach the public more reliably than public opinion reaches the administration.

2. **Cultural congruence**: Frames that align with existing cultural schemas cascade more easily. Frames that challenge cultural assumptions encounter resistance at each level.

3. **Elite dissent amplifies alternatives**: When Level 2 (other elites) actively contests Level 1 frames, Level 3 (media) is more likely to report competing frames. Elite consensus = media echo; elite dissent = media debate.

4. **Motivation matters**: Each level has actors with motivations (political, professional, commercial) that affect frame transmission. Media selection/amplification is not neutral.

5. **Feedback is weak but real**: Public opinion can affect elite behavior through polls and elections, but the signal is noisy and delayed.

## Applied Example: Iraq War Framing (Entman 2004)

- **Level 1 (Bush Administration)**: "War on Terror," "WMD threat," "Regime change"
- **Level 2 (Other Elites)**: Limited Democratic dissent pre-2003; post-2004 dissent grew
- **Level 3 (Media)**: Predominantly echoed administration frames in 2002-2003 (limited elite dissent to amplify)
- **Level 4 (News Frames)**: "Liberation," "Shock and Awe," "Mission Accomplished"
- **Level 5 (Public)**: Initial support (~70%) consistent with frame; declined as facts contradicted frame

## Conditions for Counter-Frame Success

A counter-frame can cascade upward from lower levels when:
1. Events provide clear evidence against the dominant frame
2. Credible elite defectors emerge at Level 2
3. Journalistic norms activate (investigative reporting)
4. Cultural schemas exist that make the counter-frame intelligible
5. The dominant frame's sponsors lose political capital

Without these conditions, counter-frames fail to cascade even when factually correct.

## Implications for Research

When analyzing a frame:
1. Identify which level originated the frame
2. Trace how it cascaded (or didn't) through other levels
3. Map counter-frames and their relative success
4. Assess cultural congruence as the underlying amplifier/dampener
5. Note feedback mechanisms and their effectiveness
