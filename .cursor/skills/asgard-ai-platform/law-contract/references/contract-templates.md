# Contract Templates

Common clause libraries and template shells for business contracts. These are starting-point drafts only — adapt to jurisdiction, deal specifics, and reviewed by counsel before use.

---

## Template 1: Service Agreement (B2B)

**Use when**: One party provides ongoing or project-based services to another business entity.

```
SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date")
by and between:

[VENDOR ENTITY NAME], a [state/country] [corporation/LLC/etc.] ("Vendor")
and
[CLIENT ENTITY NAME], a [state/country] [corporation/LLC/etc.] ("Client").

1. SERVICES
   Vendor shall perform the following services ("Services"):
   [DESCRIBE SERVICES WITH SPECIFICITY — avoid "consulting" alone]

   Deliverables: [LIST SPECIFIC OUTPUTS, FORMAT, ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA]
   Timeline: [MILESTONE DATES OR PROJECT SCHEDULE — attach as Exhibit A]

2. PAYMENT
   2.1 Fees. Client shall pay Vendor [AMOUNT / RATE] per [month / hour / project].
   2.2 Invoicing. Vendor shall invoice Client [monthly / upon milestone completion].
   2.3 Payment Terms. Client shall pay each invoice within [30] days of receipt.
   2.4 Late Payment. Invoices unpaid after [30] days accrue interest at [1.5%] per
       month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.
   2.5 Expenses. Client shall reimburse pre-approved expenses within [30] days
       of documented submission.

3. TERM AND TERMINATION
   3.1 Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for
       [12 months / project completion], unless earlier terminated.
   3.2 Renewal. [Option A: This Agreement does NOT auto-renew. Renewal requires
       written agreement.]
              [Option B: This Agreement auto-renews for successive [12-month] terms
       unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [60] days
       before the end of the then-current term.]
   3.3 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon
       [30] days' written notice to the other party.
   3.4 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate immediately if the other
       materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within [15] days of
       written notice.
   3.5 Effect of Termination. Client shall pay for Services rendered through the
       termination date. Sections 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9 survive termination.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
   [Option A — Client owns work product]:
   Vendor assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in deliverables created
   specifically for Client under this Agreement ("Work Product"), effective upon
   Client's full payment. Vendor retains ownership of pre-existing tools, frameworks,
   and general methodologies ("Background IP") and grants Client a non-exclusive
   license to use Background IP incorporated into Work Product.

   [Option B — Vendor owns, licenses to Client]:
   Vendor retains ownership of all Work Product. Vendor grants Client a non-exclusive,
   non-transferable license to use Work Product for Client's internal business purposes.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY
   Each party ("Receiving Party") shall keep confidential any non-public information
   disclosed by the other party ("Disclosing Party") and marked as confidential or
   that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential ("Confidential
   Information"). This obligation does not apply to information that: (a) is or
   becomes publicly known through no fault of Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully
   known before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of Confidential
   Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law (with prompt prior notice
   to Disclosing Party). This obligation survives termination for [3] years.

6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
   NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL,
   OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID
   OR PAYABLE IN THE [12] MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.
   [Carve-outs commonly added: gross negligence, willful misconduct, IP infringement,
   confidentiality breach, indemnification obligations]

7. INDEMNIFICATION
   Each party shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other from third-party
   claims arising from its own negligence, willful misconduct, or breach of this
   Agreement.

8. WARRANTIES
   Vendor warrants that: (a) Services will be performed in a professional and
   workmanlike manner; (b) Vendor has the right to grant the IP rights herein;
   (c) Work Product will not infringe third-party IP rights.
   EXCEPT AS STATED ABOVE, VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED,
   INCLUDING MERCHANTABILITY AND FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

9. GENERAL
   9.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of [JURISDICTION],
       without regard to conflict-of-law principles.
   9.2 Dispute Resolution. [Option A: Disputes shall be resolved by binding
       arbitration in [CITY] under [AAA / JAMS] rules.]
                           [Option B: The parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction
       of courts in [JURISDICTION].]
   9.3 Entire Agreement. This Agreement is the complete agreement between the
       parties and supersedes all prior negotiations.
   9.4 Amendment. No amendment is effective unless in writing signed by both parties.
   9.5 Assignment. Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's
       written consent, except to a successor in a merger or acquisition.
   9.6 Notices. Notices must be in writing, delivered to the addresses on the
       signature page.
   9.7 Severability. If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions
       continue in force.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties execute this Agreement as of the Effective Date.

[VENDOR ENTITY NAME]                    [CLIENT ENTITY NAME]

By: _____________________________       By: _____________________________
Name: ___________________________       Name: ___________________________
Title: __________________________       Title: __________________________
Date: ___________________________       Date: ___________________________
```

## Template 2: Non-Disclosure Agreement (Mutual)

**Use when**: Two parties are exploring a potential business relationship and need to share confidential information in both directions.

```
MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE]
by and between [PARTY A NAME] ("Party A") and [PARTY B NAME] ("Party B")
(each a "Party," collectively the "Parties").

PURPOSE: The Parties wish to explore [describe business purpose, e.g., "a potential
vendor relationship / partnership / acquisition"] (the "Purpose") and may disclose
Confidential Information to each other in connection with that Purpose.

1. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
   "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one
   Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other (the "Receiving Party"), whether
   orally, in writing, or by inspection of tangible items, that is designated as
   confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given
   the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

   Confidential Information does NOT include information that:
   (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;
   (b) was rightfully known to Receiving Party before disclosure;
   (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction;
   (d) is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of Confidential
       Information; or
   (e) is required to be disclosed by law or court order (with prompt written
       notice to Disclosing Party, to the extent permitted by law).

2. OBLIGATIONS
   Each Receiving Party shall:
   (a) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
   (b) protect it with at least the same care used for its own confidential
       information, but no less than reasonable care;
   (c) disclose it only to employees, contractors, or advisors who have a need
       to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective
       as this Agreement;
   (d) not reverse-engineer, disassemble, or decompile any Confidential Information.

3. TERM
   This Agreement commences on the date above and continues for [2] years.
   The confidentiality obligations survive termination for [3] additional years
   [or: indefinitely for trade secrets].

4. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION
   Upon request, Receiving Party shall promptly return or certifiably destroy all
   Confidential Information (including copies), except as required by law or
   automatically retained in backup systems.

5. NO LICENSE
   Nothing in this Agreement grants any license, right, or interest in any
   Confidential Information, patent, trademark, copyright, or other intellectual
   property except as expressly stated.

6. GENERAL
   6.1 Governing Law: [JURISDICTION]
   6.2 Entire Agreement. This is the entire agreement regarding confidentiality
       for the Purpose.
   6.3 Amendment. Writing signed by both Parties.

[PARTY A NAME]                          [PARTY B NAME]

By: _____________________________       By: _____________________________
Name: ___________________________       Name: ___________________________
Title: __________________________       Title: __________________________
Date: ___________________________       Date: ___________________________
```

## Template 3: Employment Offer Letter (with At-Will / Fixed Term)

**Use when**: Onboarding a new employee. Note: employment law is heavily jurisdiction-specific. Taiwan Labor Standards Act provisions take precedence.

```
[COMPANY LETTERHEAD]

[DATE]

[CANDIDATE NAME]
[ADDRESS]

Re: Offer of Employment

Dear [NAME],

[COMPANY NAME] ("Company") is pleased to offer you employment on the following terms:

POSITION: [JOB TITLE]
START DATE: [DATE] (subject to completion of background check / onboarding docs)
REPORTS TO: [MANAGER TITLE]
WORK LOCATION: [OFFICE ADDRESS / Remote / Hybrid — specify days]

COMPENSATION
Base Salary: [CURRENCY] [AMOUNT] per [year / month], paid [bi-weekly / monthly]
  on [payroll dates].
Bonus: [Discretionary / Target XX% of base salary] based on [Company/individual
  performance]. Not guaranteed.
Equity: [If applicable: Subject to Board approval, you will be granted [X] options /
  RSUs, vesting [4-year / 1-year cliff] schedule, per the Equity Plan.]
Benefits: [Health, dental, vision, pension/401k as described in Employee Handbook]
PTO: [X days / Unlimited PTO per policy]

EMPLOYMENT TYPE
[Option A — At-Will]:
Your employment is at-will, meaning either you or the Company may terminate the
employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice, subject to
applicable law.

[Option B — Fixed Term (Taiwan common)]:
This offer is for a fixed term of [X months], commencing [START DATE] and ending
[END DATE], renewable by mutual written agreement.

CONDITIONS
This offer is contingent upon:
(a) Satisfactory completion of a background / reference check;
(b) Your legal right to work in [JURISDICTION];
(c) Signing the Company's [Confidentiality and IP Assignment Agreement];
(d) [Any other conditions].

CONFIDENTIALITY AND IP
As a condition of employment, you will be required to sign the attached
[Employee Confidentiality and Invention Assignment Agreement].

NON-COMPETE / NON-SOLICITATION
[Include only if enforceable in the jurisdiction. Taiwan: post-employment
non-competes require: (1) legitimate protectable interest, (2) geographic/time
limits, (3) reasonable compensation to employee. Blanket bans are invalid.]

GOVERNING LAW
[JURISDICTION] law governs this offer and your employment.

This offer expires on [DATE]. Please sign and return by that date to confirm acceptance.

We look forward to having you on the team.

Sincerely,

[NAME]
[TITLE], [COMPANY NAME]

_________________________________
[CANDIDATE NAME] — Acceptance signature
Date: ____________________________
```

## Clause Library: Mix-and-Match Provisions

These clauses can be inserted into any of the templates above.

### Payment: Milestone Schedule

```
2.X Milestone Payments. The total fee of [AMOUNT] is payable as follows:

| Milestone               | Amount       | Due                          |
|-------------------------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Contract signing        | [X]% / [AMT] | Upon execution               |
| Delivery of [Phase 1]   | [X]%         | Within [10] days of delivery |
| Client acceptance of [Phase 2] | [X]% | Within [10] days of acceptance |
| Final delivery          | [X]%         | Within [10] days of final delivery |

Client shall notify Vendor of acceptance or rejection within [10] business days
of delivery. Silence constitutes acceptance.
```

### Acceptance Criteria

```
X. ACCEPTANCE
   X.1 Upon delivery of each Deliverable, Client has [10] business days to review
       and either: (a) provide written acceptance, or (b) provide written rejection
       with specific deficiencies.
   X.2 If Client rejects, Vendor has [10] business days to correct. The corrected
       Deliverable is then subject to one additional [5]-business-day review.
   X.3 If Client does not respond within the review period, the Deliverable is
       deemed accepted.
   X.4 Acceptance by Client does not waive warranty claims for latent defects
       discovered within [90] days.
```

### SLA (Service Level Agreement)

```
X. SERVICE LEVELS
   X.1 Uptime. Vendor shall maintain [99.5%] monthly uptime ("SLA"), excluding
       scheduled maintenance (maximum [4] hours/month, with [48]-hour advance notice)
       and events beyond Vendor's reasonable control.

   X.2 Uptime Calculation.
       Uptime % = (Total minutes − Downtime minutes) / Total minutes × 100

   X.3 Service Credits. If monthly uptime falls below the SLA:

       | Monthly Uptime        | Credit (% of monthly fee) |
       |-----------------------|--------------------------|
       | 99.0% – 99.4%         | 10%                      |
       | 95.0% – 98.9%         | 25%                      |
       | Below 95.0%           | 50%                      |

   X.4 Credit Cap. Credits shall not exceed 50% of the monthly fee. Credits are
       the sole remedy for SLA failures.
   X.5 Claim Process. Client must submit a credit request within [30] days of the
       incident.
```

### Force Majeure

```
X. FORCE MAJEURE
   Neither party is liable for failure or delay in performance caused by events
   beyond that party's reasonable control, including: acts of God, natural disasters,
   pandemic, war, terrorism, government action, labor strikes (not involving that
   party's employees), or failure of third-party infrastructure providers
   ("Force Majeure Event").

   The affected party shall: (a) notify the other within [5] business days;
   (b) use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate; and (c) resume performance
   promptly when the Force Majeure Event ends.

   If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [60] days, either party may
   terminate this Agreement without liability, and Client shall pay for Services
   rendered through the termination date.

   [NOTE: Pandemic, supply-chain disruption, and geopolitical events are commonly
   added explicitly post-2020. Vague "circumstances beyond our control" language
   is frequently disputed.]
```

### Governing Law + Dispute Resolution (Taiwan)

```
X. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTES
   X.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of China (Taiwan).
   X.2 The parties shall attempt to resolve disputes through good-faith negotiation
       for [30] days after written notice.
   X.3 [Option A — Court]: If unresolved, disputes shall be submitted exclusively
       to the [Taiwan Taipei District Court / Taiwan High Court] as the court of
       first instance.
   X.4 [Option B — Arbitration]: If unresolved, disputes shall be resolved by
       binding arbitration administered by the Chinese Arbitration Association,
       Taipei (CAA), under its Commercial Arbitration Rules, with proceedings
       conducted in [Mandarin / English] in Taipei. The award is final and binding.
   X.5 The prevailing party is entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and
       costs.
```

### Non-Compete (Taiwan-compliant)

```
X. POST-EMPLOYMENT NON-COMPETE
   [Use only after legal review. Taiwan Labor Standards Act Art. 9-1 requirements:]

   X.1 Scope. For [12] months after separation from employment, Employee shall not
       directly or indirectly engage in [SPECIFIC COMPETING ACTIVITY — must be
       specific, not "any competitive business"] within [GEOGRAPHIC AREA — must
       be defined].

   X.2 Legitimate Interest. Company has a legitimate interest in protecting:
       [trade secrets / customer relationships / proprietary technology — specify].

   X.3 Compensation. During the non-compete period, Company shall pay Employee
       a monthly payment of not less than [50%] of Employee's average monthly
       compensation for the last [6] months of employment. [Taiwan law requires
       "reasonable compensation" — courts have found < 33% inadequate.]

   X.4 Enforceability. If any portion of this clause exceeds limits permissible
       under applicable law, it shall be reduced to the maximum enforceable scope.

   [RED FLAG: Non-competes without compensation payments are unenforceable in
   Taiwan as of 2015 Labor Standards Act amendment. Do not omit.]
```

## Clause Comparison: One-Sided vs. Balanced

Use this table to spot vendor-drafted clauses that should be negotiated.

| Topic | Vendor-Drafted (⚠️) | Balanced Version (✓) |
|-------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Liability cap** | "Client's liability is unlimited; Vendor's liability capped at $500." | "Each party's liability capped at fees paid in prior 12 months." |
| **Indemnification** | "Client indemnifies Vendor against all claims of any kind." | "Each party indemnifies the other for its own acts/omissions." |
| **IP ownership** | "All work product belongs to Vendor; Client gets a limited license." | "Work product created specifically for Client belongs to Client upon payment." |
| **Price change** | "Vendor may change pricing at any time with 30 days' notice." | "Pricing locked for contract term; changes apply only at renewal with 60-day notice." |
| **Termination** | "Vendor may terminate any time; Client may not terminate for 12 months." | "Either party may terminate for convenience with 30-day notice." |
| **Auto-renewal** | "Agreement renews automatically; cancellation requires 90-day advance notice." | "Renewal requires affirmative written agreement; no auto-renewal." |
| **Warranty** | "Services provided AS IS; no warranties." | "Services performed in professional and workmanlike manner; work product is non-infringing." |
| **Governing law** | "Disputes in [foreign jurisdiction], [foreign language] only." | "Disputes in [local jurisdiction]; language negotiated." |
| **Data** | "Vendor may use Client data for product improvement without restriction." | "Vendor may use aggregated, anonymized data only; Client data not shared with third parties." |

## Quick-Reference: Clause Risk Checklist

Before sending any contract to the counterparty, verify:

```
FORMATION
[ ] All four elements present: offer, acceptance, consideration, legality
[ ] Correct legal entity names (not trading names or nicknames)
[ ] Signatories have actual authority (directors / officers / authorized agents)

SCOPE
[ ] Deliverables are specific, measurable, and have acceptance criteria
[ ] Timeline or milestones are defined
[ ] Change order process defined for scope changes

MONEY
[ ] Fee amount and structure unambiguous
[ ] Payment schedule explicit
[ ] Late payment remedy included
[ ] Expense reimbursement policy stated

TERM
[ ] Start date and end date / duration stated
[ ] Auto-renewal: YES or NO — stated explicitly
[ ] Termination for convenience: notice period stated
[ ] Termination for cause: cure period stated

RISK ALLOCATION
[ ] Liability cap: mutual and reasonable (typically 12-month fees)
[ ] Indemnification: mutual, not one-sided
[ ] IP ownership: clearly allocated
[ ] Warranty scope: honest but not AS-IS for everything

OPERATIONAL
[ ] Confidentiality: scope, duration, return/destruction
[ ] Governing law: known and acceptable jurisdiction
[ ] Dispute resolution: forum acceptable (court vs. arbitration)
[ ] Force majeure: covers modern risks (pandemic, supply chain)
[ ] Notices: address for legal notices specified
[ ] Entire agreement clause: prior LOIs/MOUs superseded (or carved out)
[ ] Assignment: bilateral restriction or explicit carve-out for M&A
```

## Taiwan-Specific Drafting Notes

These notes apply when one or both parties are Taiwan entities or when Taiwan law governs.

**No consideration requirement.** Taiwan Civil Code § 153 — a valid contract forms when parties agree on essential terms. Unlike common law, there is no separate "consideration" requirement. A gratuitous promise can be binding. The IRON LAW element of consideration does not apply in the same way.

**Mandatory provisions (labor contracts).** Taiwan Labor Standards Act mandates minimum terms that cannot be contracted around:
- Wage ≥ minimum wage
- Working hours ≤ statutory limits
- Overtime pay at statutory rates
- Severance / notice requirements
- Annual leave minimums

**Consumer contracts.** Consumer Protection Act applies when one party is a natural person consumer. Key implications:
- Unreasonable standard-form clauses are void (Art. 12)
- 7-day cooling-off period for certain consumer transactions (Art. 19)
- Merchant bears burden of proving clause is reasonable (Art. 11-1)

**Electronic signatures.** Electronic Signatures Act (電子簽章法) — electronic signatures and records are generally enforceable. DocuSign and equivalents are valid for most commercial contracts (exceptions: real estate transfers, wills, family law matters).

**Traditional Chinese characters.** Contracts filed with Taiwan government agencies or courts should be in Traditional Chinese. Dual-language contracts should specify which language controls in case of conflict.
