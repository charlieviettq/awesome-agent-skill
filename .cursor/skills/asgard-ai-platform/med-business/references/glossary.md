# Financial Reporting Glossary & Common Misuses

This glossary covers key terms in financial journalism, defines them precisely, and flags common mistakes that distort meaning.

---

## Accounting Bases & Scopes

### 合併報表 vs 個體報表 (Consolidated vs Individual)

- **合併報表**: Consolidated financials including parent + all subsidiaries
  - Used for: True economic picture of entire corporate group
  - Often shows higher revenue/profit due to intra-group eliminations handled
  - Standard for financial news unless otherwise specified

- **個體報表 / 個別報表**: Parent company only; excludes subsidiary earnings
  - Used for: Tax planning, dividend capacity analysis, holding-company health
  - Often lower than consolidated (especially if subs are profitable)

**Common misuse**: Mixing consolidated & individual without noting scope. Example: "Company A reported 100B revenue" (consolidated) vs "reported 80B (individual)" — two different truths.

---

### 國內 vs 國際 (Domestic vs International Operations)

- **國內營收**: Revenue from Taiwan market only
- **國際營收** / **海外營收**: Non-Taiwan revenue
- **合計**: Total from both

**Common misuse**: Omitting geographic breakdown. "Revenue grew 15%" could mean China sales boomed while Taiwan contracted. Always disclose both or note the material segment driver.

---

### 經常營業淨利 vs 稅前淨利 vs 稅後淨利

- **營業淨利**: Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold - Operating Expenses = core business profit
  - Most important for operational health assessment
  - Not affected by one-time items or financial engineering

- **業外收益** / **業外損失**: Interest, investment gains/losses, one-time items (not core operations)
  - Must be segregated and explained
  - Mark if recurring vs one-time

- **稅前淨利**: Operating profit + non-operating items - interest expenses, etc.
- **稅後淨利 / 淨利**: Final profit after tax

**Common misuse**: Reporting net profit growth without noting one-time gains inflated it. "Profit up 20%" when operating profit was only 5% and 15% came from asset sales is misleading.

---

### 合併應收帳款 vs 合併存貨

- **應收帳款** (Accounts Receivable): Money customers owe
  - Spike without revenue growth = red flag (channel stuffing, side agreements, insolvency risk)
  
- **存貨** (Inventory): Unsold goods on hand
  - Spike = demand softening, obsolescence risk, working-capital drain

**Common misuse**: Omitting these from earnings commentary. "Profit grew but receivables doubled" is a warning signal.

---

## Earnings Metrics

### EPS / 每股盈餘 (Earnings Per Share)

- **基本 EPS**: Net profit ÷ weighted-average shares outstanding
- **稀釋 EPS**: Adjusts for potential dilution (stock options, warrants, convertibles)

**Common misuse**: 
- Confusing 基本 with 稀釋
- Not noting if EPS grew due to share buybacks (not earnings growth)
- Omitting currency: "$2.50 USD EPS" ≠ "NT$2.50 EPS"

---

### ROE / 股東權益報酬率 (Return on Equity)

- **計算**: Net profit ÷ average shareholders' equity
- **解讀**: How efficiently the company converts shareholder capital into profit
- **陷阱**: High ROE + low absolute profit can occur in low-asset-base companies (service firms)

**Common misuse**: Calling ROE "high" without peer comparison. 15% ROE is mediocre in technology, excellent in retail.

---

### EBITDA / 息稅折舊前淨利

- **計算**: Net profit + Interest + Taxes + Depreciation & Amortization
- **用途**: Shows cash-earning power before financing & accounting choices
- **陷阱**: Does not reflect actual cash flow (capex, working-capital changes not included)

**Common misuse**: "EBITDA margin" sounds like profit but omits capex. High EBITDA ≠ high free cash flow.

---

## Valuation Multiples

### P/E Ratio / 本益比

- **計算**: Stock price ÷ EPS
- **解讀**: Price per dollar of earnings; higher P/E = market expects faster growth or lower risk
- **陷阱**: Not comparable across industries; not comparable if one company uses GAAP and another IFRS

**Common misuse**: "P/E of 15 is reasonable" (reasonable compared to what?). Always cite peer or historical benchmark.

---

### P/B Ratio / 股價淨值比

- **計算**: Stock price ÷ (Book value of equity per share)
- **解讀**: Price per dollar of net assets; useful for asset-heavy industries (banks, utilities)
- **陷阱**: Ignored in intangible-heavy firms (IP, software) where book value is understated

---

### PEG Ratio

- **計算**: P/E ÷ Expected earnings growth rate (%)
- **用途**: Adjusts P/E for growth expectations
- **陷阱**: Highly dependent on growth forecast accuracy; small forecast changes swing PEG wildly

## Policy & Regulatory Terms

### 重大訊息 (Material Information)

- **法律定義**: Information that could affect stock price or investor decisions
- **揭露地點**: 公開資訊觀測站 (official disclosure platform)
- **時點合規**: Must be disclosed before trading resumes; if after-hours, first thing next morning

**Journalism implication**: Use 公開資訊觀測站 timestamp, not company press-release timestamp, to mark when news became public.

---

### 內線交易 (Insider Trading) — 證交法 §157-1

- **非法行為**: Trading on material non-public information
- **風險到記者**: Publishing pre-disclosure information can expose reporter to liability under market-abuse statutes

**Journalism rule**: Never publish information before 公開資訊觀測站 official timestamp unless extremely carefully corroborated.

---

### 利益衝突 / 關係人交易 (Conflicts of Interest / Related-Party Transactions)

- **定義**: Deals between company and insiders, related firms, or entities with financial stakes
- **必須揭露**: All related-party transactions in financial statements
- **記者責任**: If company was customer/vendor to director's family firm, disclose

---

## Growth & Change Metrics

### 同比增長 / 年增 (Year-over-Year / YoY Growth)

- **計算**: (Current period - Same period last year) ÷ (Same period last year)
- **例**: 113年營收同比 +15% = 113年營收 vs 112年營收

**Common misuse**: Omitting the comparison period. "Growth of 15%" without specifying vs. prior quarter or prior year is ambiguous.

---

### 環比增長 (Quarter-over-Quarter / Sequential Growth)

- **計算**: (Current quarter - Prior quarter) ÷ (Prior quarter)
- **陷阱**: Seasonal effects can make sequential growth misleading; always compare YoY for true trend

---

### 累計 / 年初至今 (Year-to-Date / Cumulative)

- **例**: 「113年 1-6 月累計營收」vs 「113年上半年營收」(same thing)
- **差別**: 累計 = running total from start of fiscal year; not same as full-year annualized

**Common misuse**: Annualizing six-month cumulative without cautioning on seasonality. "If this trend continues, annual will be $X" requires seasonal volatility context.

---

## Currency & Valuation

### 匯率基準 (Exchange Rate Basis)

- **月底匯率**: Spot rate at month-end
- **月均匯率**: Average rate during the month
- **歷史成本**: Fixed rate used at transaction; used for balance-sheet items
- **重要性**: Currency swings can dwarf operating performance for exporters

**Common misuse**: Omitting exchange-rate basis entirely. US subsidiary earnings of "$10M" converted at 30:1 vs 35:1 is a 17% difference!

---

### 外幣轉換** (Currency Translation Effects)

- **轉換損益**: Gains/losses from revaluing foreign subsidiaries' balance sheets
- **套期損益**: Realized gains/losses from hedging contracts

**Common misuse**: Grouping all FX effect together without distinguishing economic reality from accounting noise.

## Common Abbreviations & Acronyms

| Term | Full | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| GAAP | Generally Accepted Accounting Principles | US accounting standard |
| IFRS | International Financial Reporting Standards | Global standard (used in Taiwan as of 2013) |
| FCF | Free Cash Flow | Operating cash flow - capex; real cash available |
| NPV | Net Present Value | PV of future cash flows; used in valuation |
| NPM | Net Profit Margin | Net profit ÷ Revenue |
| OCF | Operating Cash Flow | Cash generated from operations |
| WC | Working Capital | Current assets - Current liabilities |

## Quick Error Checklist

Before publishing financial numbers, ask:

- [ ] **Base period**: Is this FY 113, Q1 113, or YTD as of June 30? ← State explicitly
- [ ] **Scope**: Is this consolidated or individual? Domestic or group? ← Mark in parentheses
- [ ] **Currency**: Is this NT$ or USD? If USD, at what exchange rate? ← Always specify
- [ ] **Source**: Is this from audited year-end, unaudited quarterly, or analyst estimate? ← Tag confidence
- [ ] **One-time**: If profit spiked, did it include asset sales, restructuring, or merger gains? ← Segregate
- [ ] **Peer context**: If I say P/E 15 or ROE 12%, vs. what baseline am I comparing? ← Add benchmark
