# Ethics & Legal Red Lines for Health Journalism

## Taiwan Legal Framework

### PDPA (個人資料保護法) & Medical Privacy

**What it prohibits:**
- Publishing patient identity + medical condition without written consent
- Using personal identifiers (name, ID number, exact date of birth) in case reports
- Combining multiple data points that together allow re-identification (e.g., "60-year-old musician with rare cancer, treated in Taipei" → identifiable)

**Practical de-identification:**
- ✅ Acceptable: "A 60–65-year-old male patient with common cancer type"
- ❌ Unacceptable: "63-year-old Mr. Chen A, musician, at NTU Hospital on March 15, 2026"
- ❌ Unacceptable: Specific dates, locations, unique job titles, or medical conditions that allow identification

**Consent requirement:**
- If patient is identifiable (even partially), written informed consent required before publication
- Verbal consent is insufficient; document it
- Patient may withdraw consent before publication

### 醫療法 (Medical Care Act), Article 72

**What it protects:**
- Patient medical records, diagnoses, and medical care information
- Applies to all healthcare providers and their staff
- Covers all forms of disclosure (print, digital, verbal)

**When not applicable:**
- Public health surveillance (anonymized epidemiological data)
- Court orders or legal process
- Patient's own request

**Consequence of violation:**
- Criminal liability (罰款 NT$10,000–500,000 depending on severity)
- Civil liability (patient may sue for damages)

### 醫療廣告法 (Medical Advertising Act)

**What it restricts:**
- Claims of cure or efficacy without evidence
- Testimonials presented as evidence (insufficient for regulatory claims)
- Off-label drug use promotion

**Applies to:**
- Healthcare provider advertisements
- Drug company marketing
- News coverage that reads like promotion

**Practical red lines:**
- ❌ "Drug X cures disease" (without RCT evidence)
- ✅ "Drug X shows 50% efficacy in RCT; awaiting approval" (factual with evidence level)
- ❌ "Supplement Y is miraculous" (unproven claim)
- ✅ "Supplement Y contains ingredient Z; clinical trials pending" (statement of fact)

## Medical & Professional Ethics

### WHO Suicide Reporting Guidelines

**Four key prohibitions:**

1. **Do NOT describe the method** (hanging, drug, firearm, etc.)
   - Exception: historical/educational context acceptable if necessary
   - Rationale: Method-specific reporting increases likelihood of same method in vulnerable populations

2. **Do NOT publish location details** (hospital, park, bridge, home)
   - Exception: "In Taiwan" or "at a healthcare facility" is acceptable; specifics increase copycat risk

3. **Do NOT publish or quote suicide notes or written messages**
   - Exception: Single phrase for news value only if journalist/editor judgment indicates critical public interest

4. **Do NOT glorify or romanticize** the deceased or the act
   - Use neutral language: "died by suicide" (not "committed suicide", "successful attempt", or "tragic hero")
   - Avoid glorifying language: "courageous", "liberating", "final escape"

**What you MUST include:**

- **Helpline numbers (with context)**: 
  - Taiwan: 1925 (安心專線), 1626 (張老師), 1999 (撒瑪利亞防止自殺會)
  - International: WHO Suicide Prevention Resource List
  - Format: "If you are in crisis, contact [org] at [number]"

- **Brief risk factors (if newsworthy)**: 
  - Depression, substance abuse, recent loss, chronic illness
  - Avoid suggesting causation without evidence

- **Resources for vulnerable populations**:
  - Mental health services, crisis counseling, support groups

**Example formatting:**

```
❌ WRONG: "Mr. Chen, 45, hanged himself in the Taipei Bridge at 
midnight after leaving a note saying 'I cannot bear this anymore'."

✅ RIGHT: "A 45-year-old man died by suicide. He had recently 
experienced job loss and depression. If you are in crisis, contact 
the Emotional Support Hotline at 1925 (24 hours, free)."
```

**Consequence of violation:**
- Documentedwerther effect (copycat suicides in following weeks)
- Professional liability; potential civil/criminal charges under 傳染病防治法 if impact on public health is demonstrated
- Institutional reputation damage

### Researcher / Expert Conflict of Interest

**Must disclose when citing:**

1. **Financial interest** (ongoing):
   - Stock ownership in pharmaceutical company
   - Research funding from company
   - Consulting fees or advisory board membership
   - Patent royalties

2. **Institutional conflict**:
   - Hospital that profits from the drug/treatment being discussed
   - University that receives endowment from pharma company

3. **Social/professional conflict**:
   - Close personal relationship with researcher
   - Competitive rivalry with a researcher mentioned in opposing view

**How to disclose:**
- Integrate into first mention: "Dr. Wang, who received consulting fees from Pharma Company X, stated..."
- Or footnote if space is limited: "Footnote: Dr. Wang received $50,000 in consulting fees from Pharma Corp in 2024"
- Never bury in small print

**Example:**
```
❌ "Dr. Wang states the drug is effective."
✅ "Dr. Wang, who received research funding from the drug's manufacturer, 
    states the drug is effective based on his trial data."
```

## Defamation & Accuracy

### 刑法 (Criminal Code) 310 — Defamation

**Taiwan defamation is broader than US:**
- Truth is **not an absolute defense** (unlike 1A in US)
- Must prove: (1) statement is true, (2) published, (3) identifies person, (4) damages reputation
- If all four met, truth is a defense only if publication was in "public interest"
- "Public interest" is narrowly interpreted; "interesting to public" ≠ "public interest"

**Health journalism red lines:**
- ❌ "Drug X is dangerous" (without specific adverse-event data)
- ✅ "The FDA received 100 reports of side effect Y from Drug X; investigation ongoing"
- ❌ "Hospital Y provides poor care" (generalized claim)
- ✅ "Hospital Y had 15 infection-control violations in 2024, per health inspection records"

**Medical accusation-specific protocol:**
1. Obtain documents/records (not hearsay)
2. Contact accused for response; allow reasonable time
3. State response opportunity: "Hospital Y was contacted multiple times between [date] and [date]; declined to comment"
4. Publish only with corroboration (avoid single-source accusations)

## Special-Category Topics

### Minors (兒少法 — Child and Youth Protection Act)

- No publication of identifying information (name, photo, institution) without guardian consent
- No graphic description of child abuse
- Court orders may restrict reporting; verify before publication

### Sexual Assault Victims (性侵害犯罪防治法)

- Absolutely no publication of victim identity
- No victim photo or any identifying information
- Penalties severe (criminal liability); never risk this

### Infectious Disease Reporting (傳染病防治法)

- Early numbers often preliminary; state explicitly: "as of [date], [official source] reports"
- Do not imply causation without evidence (e.g., "after vaccine" ≠ "caused by vaccine")
- Report both cases and rates (case count alone can mislead)

## Institutional Transparency & Fairness

### Right of Response

**When making an allegation or claim against a named organization/person:**
1. Contact the accused or their official representative
2. Provide clear timeline and specificity: "We are asking about your response to X allegation regarding Y incident on Z date"
3. Allow reasonable time (typically 24–48 hours for news; longer for complex topics)
4. Document attempts: "Contacted Dr. Chen via email on April 25, via phone April 26; no response by press time"
5. State non-response in published piece: "Contacted X; no response as of publication"

### Anonymous Source Protocol

**For negative claims against named entities:**
1. Source must face real, identifiable risk of harm (retaliation, job loss)
2. Editor must approve anonymity (not reporter's unilateral decision)
3. Corroboration required (don't publish allegation on single anonymous source)
4. Reason for anonymity stated in piece: "who requested anonymity to avoid retaliation"

**Example:**
```
❌ "A source said the hospital falsified safety records."
✅ "A hospital staff member, who requested anonymity for fear of 
    job retaliation, stated that safety records were falsified. 
    The hospital declined to comment."
```

## Advertising & Sponsorship Transparency

### Native Advertising / Sponsored Content

- Must be clearly labeled as "Sponsored" or "Advertisement"
- Cannot appear to be news reporting
- Same ethical standards apply as paid ads

### Medical Device / Pharmaceutical Mentions

- If article mentions product by brand name:
  - Cite evidence level (RCT, case report, etc.)
  - Include indication & side effects
  - Disclose any payment to publication or journalist from manufacturer

## Consent & Informed Participation

### Patient Interview Consent

**Written consent should cover:**
1. Scope of interview (topic, expected quotes)
2. Whether name/identifying information will be used
3. Right to review quotes before publication (optional; not standard but builds trust)
4. How interview will be used (news article, audio, video, etc.)
5. Right to withdraw before publication (date-stamped)

### Vulnerable Populations

- Minors: guardian + child assent (if age-appropriate)
- Mental illness: ensure capacity to consent; consider whether participation might harm
- Addiction: informed consent about privacy risk
