# Sources and Beats: Health News Beat Map

## Taiwan Government Health Authorities (Tier 1)

| Agency | Scope | How to access | Notes |
|--------|-------|---------------|-------|
| **衛生福利部 (Ministry of Health and Welfare)** | Policy, budget, nationwide health initiatives | https://www.mohw.gov.tw | Press releases, public consultation records, annual reports |
| **疾病管制署 (CDC Taiwan)** | Communicable disease surveillance, epidemiology, vaccines, emergency response | https://www.cdc.gov.tw | Weekly disease surveillance report (疫情週報), press briefings during outbreaks |
| **食品藥物管理署 (FDA Taiwan)** | Drug/device approval, adverse event reporting (Vigil system), drug pricing | https://www.fda.gov.tw | New drug approvals, safety alerts (藥物安全資訊), post-market surveillance |
| **國民健康署 (National Health Bureau)** | Prevention, screening programs, health promotion | https://www.hpa.gov.tw | Cancer screening guidelines, vaccination schedules, public health campaigns |
| **健保署 (National Health Insurance Administration)** | Coverage decisions, reimbursement policy, healthcare cost data | https://www.nhi.gov.tw | Press releases on new covered treatments, payment reform announcements |
| **國家衛生研究院 (National Health Research Institutes)** | Research grants, epidemiological studies, health policy analysis | https://www.nhri.org.tw | Research findings, policy briefs, seminars |

## Key News Beats & Story Types

| Beat | Primary Sources | Red Flags |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Infectious Disease / Outbreak** | CDC Taiwan, 疾管署, WHO, 醫院感管 | Early numbers often revised; avoid implying causation (e.g., vaccine → event) |
| **Drug Approval** | FDA Taiwan (食藥署), clinical trial sponsors, medical societies | Conflict of interest (trial funded by pharma); side-effect reporting gaps |
| **Clinical Research** | Journal articles, university press releases, researcher interviews | Single-study overreach; preprint vs peer-reviewed status |
| **Health Policy** | 衛福部, 健保署, legislative hearings | Policy intent vs real-world implementation; affected population |
| **Screening / Prevention** | 衛生福利部, medical societies, epidemiological data | Lead-time bias (early detection ≠ survival improvement) |
| **Patient Stories** | Hospital patient affairs, patient advocacy groups, direct outreach | De-identification; generalizability limits; selection bias |

## Sourcing Strategy

**Tier 1 verification (before publication):**
1. Get primary government statement or journal article, not secondary relay
2. For drugs/approvals: verify in FDA Taiwan (食藥署) public database
3. For epidemiology: cross-check case numbers with 疾管署 official data
4. For clinical claims: read abstract/methods, don't rely on press release alone

**Tier 2 verification (rapid fact-check):**
1. Medical society official statement (e.g., 心臟學會) on clinical recommendations
2. Researcher direct quote (not paraphrased by university PR)
3. Independent expert commentary (outside trial institution)

**Red flags (stop and verify):**
- "Study shows X is 'dangerous' / 'miracle cure'" (superlatives without data)
- Single case report framed as pattern
- Pharmaceutical company press release as sole source
- Anonymous source without risk justification
