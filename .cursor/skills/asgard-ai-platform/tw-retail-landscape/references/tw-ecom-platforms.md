# Taiwan E-Commerce Platform Comparison

## Platform Overview

Taiwan's e-commerce market (~NT$500B+, growing 15-20% YoY) is dominated by four platforms with distinct positioning, fee structures, and customer profiles.

| Platform | GMV Rank | Strength | Primary Category |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| **momo購物網** | #1 | Speed, trust, logistics | Electronics, beauty, FMCG |
| **蝦皮 (Shopee TW)** | #2 | Price, mobile-first, SEA synergy | Fashion, daily goods, C2C |
| **PChome 24h** | #3 | Electronics depth, 24h delivery SLA | Electronics, 3C, home appliances |
| **樂天市場 (Rakuten TW)** | #4 | Japan-brand halo, loyalty points | Japanese goods, beauty, food |

## Platform Deep Dives

### momo購物網

**Operator**: 富邦媒體科技 (Fubon Media Technology)  
**Monthly active users**: ~14M  
**Key advantage**: Own logistics network (momo快速到貨) + TV shopping channel cross-promotion

**Fee structure**:
| Fee type | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Commission | 4–15% (category dependent) |
| Monthly store fee | NT$0–3,000 (tier-based) |
| Promotional slot fee | NT$5,000–200,000+ per campaign |
| Return logistics subsidy | Negotiated (brand-specific) |

**Commission by category (approximate)**:
| Category | Commission |
|----------|-----------|
| Electronics / 3C | 4–6% |
| Beauty / personal care | 10–13% |
| Food / FMCG | 8–12% |
| Fashion / apparel | 10–15% |
| Home / furniture | 6–10% |

**What momo buyers look like**: 30–50 female demographic dominant; high average order value (NT$800–1,200); strong repurchase rate; expects next-day delivery as default.

**Critical insight**: momo's algorithm strongly rewards sellers who participate in its promotional calendars (see Seasonality section below). Brands that opt out of 週年慶 deals see significant organic ranking drops for 4–6 weeks afterward.

---

### 蝦皮購物 (Shopee Taiwan)

**Operator**: Sea Limited (Singapore-listed, SEA regional operator)  
**Monthly active users**: ~16M (largest raw traffic, but lower AOV)  
**Key advantage**: Zero commission during aggressive growth phases; mobile app with addictive gamification; C2C + B2C hybrid

**Fee structure** (as of 2024; Shopee changes fees frequently):
| Fee type | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Transaction fee | 2–6% (category dependent) |
| Payment processing | 2–2.5% |
| Marketing (ShopeeMall brands) | Additional 1–3% |
| ShopeeMall joining fee | NT$0 (covered by required security deposit NT$30,000–100,000) |

**Commission by category (approximate)**:
| Category | Transaction fee |
|----------|----------------|
| Electronics | 2% |
| Beauty | 5% |
| Fashion | 5–6% |
| Food / FMCG | 3–4% |
| Home | 4% |

**What Shopee buyers look like**: Price-sensitive; younger demographic (20–35); mobile-only; high browse-to-purchase ratio but lower conversion; loves free shipping + vouchers; comfortable buying from individual sellers.

**Critical insight**: Shopee's search ranking algorithm is heavily influenced by sales velocity in the first 72 hours of a listing, review count, and response time. New listings must run Shopee Ads (CPC) to generate initial velocity — organic discovery for new SKUs is nearly impossible without paid support.

**ShopeeMall vs. Marketplace**: ShopeeMall (brand-verified) gets blue badge, dedicated search filter, and higher trust signals. Required for brands with NT$1M+/month target. Requires business registration, product authenticity guarantee, and stricter return policy compliance.

---

### PChome 24h

**Operator**: PChome Online (台灣大型本土電商，TWSE listed)  
**Monthly active users**: ~8M  
**Key advantage**: 24-hour delivery guarantee (Taipei metro) is a unique SLA in market; strong 3C/electronics credibility

**Fee structure**:
| Fee type | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Commission | 3–12% |
| Monthly listing fee | NT$1,500–5,000 |
| Warehousing (PChome fulfillment) | Per-unit + storage fees |
| Payment processing | Included in commission |

**PChome's unique model**: PChome operates a **買斷 (buy-out) model** for top sellers — PChome purchases inventory outright and becomes the seller of record. Margin expectations are 30–40% off MSRP. This differs fundamentally from Shopee/momo's marketplace commission model.

For brands not on the buy-out model, PChome's marketplace (商店街) is smaller and less trafficked.

**What PChome buyers look like**: Male-skewed (55%+); 30–45 age range; high 3C and electronics knowledge; values the 24h SLA; less price-sensitive than Shopee but less brand-loyal than momo.

**Critical insight**: PChome 24h guarantee requires seller to maintain inventory in PChome's warehouse (北部物流中心). This creates capital requirement — brands must pre-stock, accepting write-off risk. PChome will delist SKUs that cannot meet the 24h SLA if inventory runs out.

### 樂天市場 Taiwan (Rakuten Ichiba TW)

**Operator**: Rakuten Taiwan (subsidiary of Japanese Rakuten Group)  
**Monthly active users**: ~3M  
**Key advantage**: Japan-brand authenticity signal; Rakuten Super Points loyalty ecosystem; Japanese aesthetics and product curation

**Fee structure**:
| Fee type | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Monthly store fee | NT$4,500–25,000 (by store tier) |
| Commission | 3.5–8% |
| Payment processing | 1.5–2.5% |
| R-Points subsidy (required) | 1% minimum point-back |

**What Rakuten buyers look like**: Japan-brand enthusiasts; 30–50 female; higher disposable income; willing to pay premium for authentic Japanese goods; highly loyal to the Rakuten Points ecosystem.

**Critical insight**: Rakuten TW is the right platform if your brand has a Japan connection (licensed, imported, or Japan-style). Without that, conversion rates are significantly lower than momo or Shopee despite similar traffic investment.

## Platform Selection Decision Framework

Use this decision table to determine primary vs. secondary platform:

### Step 1: Category Fit Filter

| Your category | Best primary | Avoid (low ROI) |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 3C / Electronics | PChome > momo | Rakuten |
| Beauty / Skincare | momo > Shopee | PChome |
| Fashion / Apparel | Shopee > momo | PChome |
| Japanese goods | Rakuten > momo | Shopee |
| Food / FMCG | momo > Shopee | PChome |
| Home / Furniture | momo > PChome | Rakuten |
| Baby / Parenting | momo > Shopee | PChome |

### Step 2: Budget + Margin Threshold Check

Calculate **minimum viable margin** before listing:

```
Net margin per unit =
  MSRP
  − (MSRP × commission_rate)
  − (MSRP × payment_processing_rate)
  − logistics_cost_per_unit
  − promotional_allowance_per_unit   ← budget 5–15% of MSRP
  − return_cost × return_rate        ← assume 3–8% for most categories
  − COGS
```

**Rule of thumb**: If net margin < 15% of MSRP at realistic sell-through with promotions, the platform unit economics are broken. Do not list and expect to fix it with volume.

**Worked example** (beauty product, MSRP NT$600, COGS NT$150):

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| MSRP | NT$600 |
| momo commission (12%) | −NT$72 |
| Payment processing (included) | NT$0 |
| Logistics (momo ECO box) | −NT$65 |
| Promotional allowance (10%) | −NT$60 |
| Return cost (5% rate × NT$130) | −NT$6.5 |
| COGS | −NT$150 |
| **Net margin** | **NT$246.5 (41%)** |

→ 41% net margin = viable. Proceed.

Repeat for Shopee (lower commission, but higher logistics subsidy expected, lower AOV):

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| MSRP (effective after vouchers) | NT$520 |
| Shopee transaction fee (5%) | −NT$26 |
| Payment processing (2.3%) | −NT$12 |
| Logistics (free shipping expected) | −NT$80 |
| Shopee Ads (CPC budget) | −NT$52 (10% of revenue) |
| Return cost (7% × NT$130) | −NT$9 |
| COGS | −NT$150 |
| **Net margin** | **NT$191 (37%)** |

→ 37% — viable, but ad spend is load-bearing. If CPC costs rise, recalculate.

### Step 3: Brand Stage Mapping

| Brand stage | Recommended platform priority |
|-------------|-------------------------------|
| New to market, unknown brand | Shopee first (lower barrier, fast volume test) |
| Established brand, building trust | momo primary (trust signal, reach) |
| Premium / high-AOV brand | momo + department store online (太平洋、新光三越) |
| 3C / electronics brand | PChome primary, momo secondary |
| Japan-origin or Japan-style | Rakuten primary, momo secondary |
| D2C / subscription model | Own website + LINE官方帳號 (platforms take margin on every order) |

## Promotional Calendar — Must-Participate Events

Missing these events means losing algorithmic favor AND market share during peak buying windows.

| Event | Timing | Key platform | GMV impact |
|-------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| 春節前購物 | Jan (2 weeks before CNY) | All | High |
| 婦女節 | Late Feb – early Mar | momo, Shopee | Medium (beauty) |
| 618購物節 | Jun 1–18 | Shopee dominant | High |
| 雙11 | Nov 1–11 | All | Highest of year |
| 雙12 | Dec 1–12 | Shopee, momo | High |
| momo週年慶 | Oct–Nov (varies) | momo | High for momo brands |
| 百貨週年慶 online | Sep–Nov | Department store online | High for premium |

**Planning rule**: For 雙11 and 618, submit promotional deals 6–8 weeks in advance. Platforms require price lock-in (deal price ≤ lowest price in prior 30 days) — discounting before the event to "reset" baseline is a policy violation and can result in delisting.

## Logistics: CVS Pickup Integration (超商取貨)

As noted in the IRON LAW: ~30% of Taiwan e-commerce deliveries go to CVS. All major platforms integrate CVS pickup:

| Platform | CVS pickup partners | Buyer pays? | COD available? |
|----------|--------------------|-----------| --------------|
| momo | 7-ELEVEN, FamilyMart | NT$60–80 | Yes (with COD fee) |
| Shopee | 7-ELEVEN, FamilyMart, Hi-Life | NT$60–80 | Yes |
| PChome | 7-ELEVEN | NT$70 | No |
| Rakuten | 7-ELEVEN, FamilyMart | NT$60–80 | No |

**Seller implication**: Products over 5kg or with longest dimension > 45cm are ineligible for CVS pickup. If your product exceeds these limits, you lose access to ~30% of Taiwan's preferred delivery method — price in the conversion rate impact.

**COD (貨到付款) note**: COD is disproportionately popular in Taiwan compared to other developed markets (~15% of CVS pickup orders). It reduces fraud risk for buyers but increases operational complexity (7–14 day payment float, higher return rate on unopened COD packages). Evaluate by category: fashion has 25%+ COD rate; electronics 5–10%.

## Cross-Platform Pricing Policy

Taiwan platform algorithms detect and react to cross-platform pricing gaps. Key rules:

1. **Price parity is expected but not always enforceable**: momo and Shopee both have MFN (most-favored-nation) clauses in their contracts — your price on their platform must match or beat your lowest price elsewhere (including your own website).

2. **Violating price parity**: Platforms demote listings when they detect cheaper prices on competitor platforms via automated price scraping. The demotion is algorithmic — you will not receive a warning.

3. **Managing channel conflict**: Use bundle/kit SKUs (e.g., "2-pack" or "kit with accessories") to create price-comparable products that aren't directly comparable across platforms. This is the standard workaround.

4. **MSRP vs. promotional price**: Set MSRP correctly from day one. Running constant deep discounts (>30% off MSRP) trains buyers to expect low prices and destroys brand equity. Budget promotions as a % of annual revenue, not as an emergency tool.

## Platform-Specific SEO / Search Ranking Factors

Each platform's search algorithm has different weights:

### momo
- Sales velocity (units sold in last 7 days): ~35% weight
- Review score and count: ~25%
- Participation in platform promotions: ~20%
- Listing completeness (images, specs, keywords): ~15%
- Return rate penalty: −10% if return rate >8%

### Shopee
- Conversion rate (listing CTR × add-to-cart × purchase): ~30%
- Sales velocity (last 30 days): ~25%
- Shopee Ads (paid placement bleeds into organic ranking): ~20%
- Response time (must be <12 hours to avoid penalty): ~10%
- Review count + Liked count: ~15%

### PChome
- 24h SLA compliance: binary (must maintain or lose listing)
- Price competitiveness vs. other PChome sellers: ~40%
- Sales volume: ~35%
- Listing quality: ~25%

## Common Mistakes

**Launching on all platforms simultaneously**: Spreading budget and operations across 4 platforms means none gets the sales velocity needed to rank. Launch one platform, prove unit economics, then expand.

**Ignoring platform-specific image requirements**: momo requires white background main image; Shopee allows lifestyle first image; PChome requires spec table in images. Using the same asset pack across all platforms reduces conversion.

**Setting logistics to home delivery only**: Removing CVS pickup option reduces buyer pool by ~30%. Always enable CVS pickup if product dimensions allow.

**Treating Shopee as a dumping ground for excess inventory**: Shopee buyers cross-reference prices. Discounting old inventory on Shopee will trigger price parity enforcement on momo and may damage brand perception.

**Misreading Rakuten TW traffic as Japan opportunity**: Rakuten TW buyers are Taiwan residents who prefer Japanese-style products. They are not Japanese consumers. Shipping from Japan is still subject to Taiwan customs duties — misdeclaring value is illegal.
