# Content Calendar

## Core Principle

A content calendar is a scheduling system, not a creative brief. Its job is to enforce the 80/20 rule mechanically: across any 4-week period, no more than 20% of posts should be promotional. The calendar enforces this before you write a single word.

---

## The 4-Week Rotation Model

Use a repeating 4-week block. Every week has the same structural role; the topics rotate.

```
Week 1: Authority
Week 2: Value
Week 3: Trust
Week 4: Harvest (engagement + soft CTA)
```

Within each week, use the 5-day rhythm from SKILL.md:

| Day | Content Type       | Tone           | CTA Style          |
|-----|--------------------|----------------|--------------------|
| Mon | Industry insight   | Authoritative  | Thought-provoking question |
| Tue | Tutorial / how-to  | Instructional  | Save / share       |
| Wed | Behind-the-scenes  | Warm, human    | Ask for reaction   |
| Thu | User story / UGC   | Social proof   | Tag someone        |
| Fri | Poll / casual      | Playful        | Vote / react       |

Weekend posts are optional. If you post on weekends, default to resharing top-performing weekday content with a new caption angle — do not produce new content types on weekends.

---

## Promotional Post Quota

Count promotional posts separately. Across a standard 20 weekday slots per month:

```
Max promotional slots = 20 × 0.20 = 4 posts/month
```

Promotional = any post whose primary goal is to sell a product, promote a service, or drive direct conversion. Thought leadership that happens to mention your product is **not** promotional.

Reserve promotional slots for:
1. Product launch or feature announcement
2. Limited-time offer or event
3. Case study with direct conversion link
4. Pricing/package announcement

If you run out of quota, push the promotional post to next month. Do not exceed the cap.

---

## Platform Allocation per Slot

Not every content type belongs on every platform. Use this allocation matrix to decide which platforms get each day's post:

| Day Type              | Instagram | LinkedIn | X/Twitter | Facebook |
|-----------------------|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Industry insight      | ◐         | ●        | ●         | ○        |
| Tutorial / how-to     | ●         | ◐        | ○         | ◐        |
| Behind-the-scenes     | ●         | ◐        | ○         | ●        |
| User story / UGC      | ●         | ◐        | ○         | ●        |
| Poll / casual         | ◐         | ○        | ●         | ◐        |
| Promotional           | ◐         | ●        | ◐         | ◐        |

● = primary platform (write a native version)  
◐ = secondary platform (adapt from primary, do not copy-paste)  
○ = skip or repurpose only if capacity allows

**IRON LAW reinforcement**: "adapt" means rewriting the hook and adjusting tone. A LinkedIn industry insight uses data and professional framing; the same topic on X/Twitter becomes a sharp one-liner or thread starter. Never copy-paste the same caption across platforms.

## Scheduling Mechanics

### Optimal Post Times (Taiwan / GMT+8)

Based on typical B2C and B2B patterns for Taiwan audience. Adjust based on your own analytics after 60 days.

| Platform  | Weekday best windows          | Weekend       |
|-----------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| Instagram | 12:00–13:00, 19:00–21:00      | 10:00–12:00   |
| LinkedIn  | 08:00–09:00, 12:00–13:00      | Avoid         |
| X/Twitter | 08:00–09:00, 21:00–23:00      | 10:00–12:00   |
| Facebook  | 13:00–15:00, 20:00–22:00      | 10:00–12:00   |

The LinkedIn morning window (08:00–09:00) is particularly strong for Taiwan professionals commuting or checking feeds before work. Miss this window and reach drops noticeably.

### Buffer Before First Comment

Post → wait 10 minutes → add your own comment with:
- The link you couldn't put in the post body (LinkedIn, Instagram)
- A follow-up question if engagement is the goal
- On Instagram: the bulk of your hashtags if you prefer cleaner captions

This is not optional for LinkedIn — a link in the post body is penalized by the algorithm. Put it in the first comment.

## Monthly Planning Workflow

Run this process on the last Thursday of each month for the following month.

**Step 1 — Review last month's performance** (30 min)

Pull these numbers for each post:
- Reach / impressions
- Engagement rate = (likes + comments + shares + saves) / reach
- Profile visits or link clicks (if applicable)

Flag the top 3 posts by engagement rate. Note what content type, hook technique, and platform they were on. These are your signals for the next month.

**Step 2 — Set topics** (45 min)

Fill in the 20 weekday slots with topics only — no captions yet. Topics should come from:
- Product roadmap events (launches, updates, milestones)
- Industry calendar (conferences, regulatory changes, Taiwan holidays)
- Your top-performing content from last month (repurpose with new angle)
- Evergreen tutorials that can rotate annually

Do not fill topics from inspiration or vague ideas. Every topic needs a source.

**Step 3 — Assign promotional slots** (10 min)

Identify which 4 slots (max) will be promotional. Mark them clearly. If you have fewer than 4 promotional needs this month, leave the remaining slots as value content — do not manufacture promotions to fill the quota.

**Step 4 — Write copy** (varies)

Write in batches by platform, not by date. Write all LinkedIn posts in one session, then Instagram, then X/Twitter. Batching keeps your voice consistent within each platform.

**Step 5 — Schedule** (30 min)

Load into your scheduling tool. Confirm the first-comment plan for any LinkedIn posts with links.

## Evergreen vs. Timely Split

Aim for 60/40 across a month:

```
Evergreen: 60% of posts (tutorials, frameworks, UGC, behind-the-scenes)
Timely: 40% of posts (industry news, product updates, events, trends)
```

Evergreen posts can be written 2–3 weeks ahead. Timely posts need a 48-hour turnaround window — do not schedule timely posts more than 1 week ahead or they may be stale when they publish.

If a major news event happens, swap a timely slot from your calendar into that week. Have one blank "reserve" slot per week in your calendar specifically for reactive content.

---

## Repost and Repurpose Rules

| Content type          | Repost window       | Adaptation required          |
|-----------------------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| Evergreen tutorial    | 90 days minimum     | New hook line, update stats  |
| Behind-the-scenes     | Do not repost       | —                            |
| UGC / user story      | 6 months minimum    | Different framing angle      |
| Industry insight      | 60 days minimum     | Add new data point or update |
| Poll                  | 30 days minimum     | Change the options           |

A repurposed post is not a lazy post. Change the hook entirely. If the original hook was a question, make the repurposed version a bold claim. Different hooks reach different segments of your audience even on the same platform.

---

## Warning Signs in Your Calendar

If any of these are true, fix the calendar before writing copy:

- **More than 2 consecutive days of the same content type** — algorithm diversification suffers; audience fatigues faster
- **No UGC or social proof in Week 3 or 4** — the trust phase has no evidence; claims feel hollow
- **Promotional posts in Week 1** — you haven't earned the ask yet; authority and value come first
- **All posts on one platform** — cross-platform diversification is a reach hedge; single-platform strategy creates fragile dependency
- **Friday post is promotional** — Friday is for engagement and fun; promotional on Friday performs worst across all platforms consistently
