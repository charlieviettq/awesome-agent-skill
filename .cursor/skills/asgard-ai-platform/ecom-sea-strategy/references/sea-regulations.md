# SEA E-Commerce Regulations by Country

> **Core Warning**: Regulations in SEA change frequently and enforcement is inconsistent. Verify with local legal counsel before committing capital. This document reflects conditions as of early 2026.

---

## Indonesia

### Business Entity Requirement

Foreign companies **cannot sell directly** on Indonesian marketplaces in most categories without a local entity. Two main structures:

| Structure | Setup Time | Minimum Capital | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|-------|
| PT PMA (Foreign-Owned LLC) | 3-6 months | IDR 10 billion (~USD 620K) for general trading | Required for retail trade |
| Representative Office | 2-3 months | None | Cannot generate revenue; marketing/liaison only |
| Local distributor/partner | 2-4 weeks | None (your partner's cost) | Most common for market entry |

**Practical entry path for most foreign brands**: Partner with an Indonesian distributor who operates the marketplace store under their local entity. You supply inventory and set brand guidelines; they handle regulatory exposure.

### Product Categories Requiring Special Permits

| Category | Permit | Issuing Body | Time to Obtain |
|----------|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Food & beverages | BPOM registration | Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan | 3-12 months |
| Cosmetics | BPOM notification | BPOM | 2-6 months |
| Supplements | BPOM registration | BPOM | 6-18 months |
| Electronics | SNI certification | BSN / Ministry of Industry | 3-6 months |
| Medical devices | Izin Edar | Kemenkes | 12-24 months |
| Toys | SNI certification | BSN | 3-6 months |

### Import & Customs

- **De minimis threshold**: USD 3 per shipment (extremely low). Most cross-border shipments attract import duty + VAT.
- **Import duty rates**: 0-40% depending on HS code. Fashion/apparel often 15-25%.
- **VAT**: 11% on all goods.
- **PPN (VAT) for digital services**: Foreign digital platforms must register for PPN if revenue > IDR 600M/year from Indonesian users.
- **Lartas**: Certain goods have import restrictions (prohibited/restricted list). Check at INSW (Indonesian National Single Window).

### E-Commerce Specific Rules (GR 80/2019)

- Marketplace operators must verify seller identity and product legality.
- Cross-border e-commerce sellers must be registered if selling directly to consumers.
- Consumer data must follow personal data protection requirements (PDP Law 2022, effective Oct 2024).

## Vietnam

### Foreign Ownership Restrictions

Vietnam restricts foreign investment in retail and e-commerce distribution. Key rules:

- Foreign-invested enterprises (FIEs) can sell online but may need Economic Needs Test (ENT) approval for physical retail.
- For pure e-commerce (no physical store): FIEs can own up to 100% in many cases, but must register with Ministry of Industry and Trade (MOIT).
- **MOIT e-commerce registration**: Required for any website/app selling to Vietnamese consumers. Process: 3-7 business days via online portal (`online.gov.vn`).

### E-Commerce Notification vs. License

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Personal/small-scale seller | No registration required |
| Revenue ≥ VND 100M/year (~USD 4K) | Must register for tax |
| Operating an e-commerce website | MOIT notification (mandatory) |
| Operating a marketplace (C2C/B2C platform) | MOIT license (more extensive) |

### Tax Obligations for Foreign Sellers

Vietnam introduced the Foreign Contractor Tax (FCT) and requires foreign sellers with Vietnamese-sourced income to pay:

- **FCT on goods**: 1% on turnover (if not VAT registered locally)
- **VAT**: 10% standard rate; 0% for exports
- Shopee VN and Lazada VN are required to **withhold and remit** tax on behalf of foreign sellers for cross-border transactions starting 2022.

**Practical implication**: If you sell from Taiwan on Shopee VN, Shopee deducts FCT before remitting your payout. Factor this into margin calculations.

### COD Regulatory Nuance

Vietnam Post and licensed logistics companies are the only entities legally permitted to handle COD collection. Unlicensed cash handling = regulatory risk. Verify your 3PL has proper licensing before launching COD.

### Consumer Protection

- Complaint resolution deadline: 3 business days for marketplace sellers.
- Return policy must be disclosed before purchase; no-return policies are unenforceable for defective goods.
- Product recall obligations apply to all sellers regardless of origin.

## Thailand

### Business Structure

Thailand's Foreign Business Act (FBA) restricts foreigners in List 3 businesses, which includes retail and wholesale. Workarounds:

| Option | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Thai-majority company | 51%+ Thai ownership; foreigner can hold preferred shares with special rights |
| BOI promotion | Board of Investment promotion allows 100% foreign ownership in qualifying tech/manufacturing sectors; not typically applicable to pure retail |
| Thai distributor | Most practical for market entry; similar to Indonesia approach |
| Treaty of Amity (US companies only) | US entities can own 100% in most sectors except FBA List 2 |

### Marketplace Selling without Local Entity

For **cross-border sales on Shopee TH / Lazada TH**: Platforms may allow foreign sellers in their cross-border programs without requiring a Thai entity. However:

- Customs clearance required for each shipment.
- Import duty applies above THB 1,500 de minimis (~USD 42).
- VAT: 7% (currently); collected by marketplace for qualifying transactions.

### VAT Registration

- Required when annual revenue from Thailand exceeds THB 1.8M (~USD 50K).
- Foreign e-commerce businesses exceeding this threshold must register with Revenue Department.
- Shopee TH and Lazada TH are required to collect and remit VAT on behalf of foreign sellers for qualifying cross-border transactions (since Sep 2021).

### Social Commerce Regulations

Thailand has informal but increasingly enforced rules on influencer marketing and live commerce:

- Price claims ("lowest price", "50% off") must be accurate; enforced by Consumer Protection Board (CPB).
- Health/beauty claims require pre-approval from FDA Thailand for advertising.
- No specific live-streaming law yet, but consumer protection laws apply to all sales channels.

## Philippines

### E-Commerce Act & DTI Registration

Republic Act 8792 (E-Commerce Act) governs electronic transactions. For sellers:

- Philippine-based business: DTI registration for sole proprietors; SEC registration for corporations.
- **Foreign sellers**: No mandatory registration to sell cross-border, but must comply with:
  - Bureau of Customs (BOC) rules for imports
  - Consumer Act (RA 7394) protections

### Import Thresholds

| Shipment Value | Duty Status |
|---------------|-------------|
| ≤ PHP 10,000 (~USD 175) | De minimis — duty-free |
| > PHP 10,000 | Subject to customs duty + 12% VAT |

**Note**: The PHP 10,000 de minimis is among the higher thresholds in SEA, making it relatively favorable for small cross-border shipments.

### Restricted & Prohibited Products

- **Prohibited**: counterfeit goods, endangered species products, certain firearms
- **Restricted** (require permits): food, cosmetics, supplements (FDA Philippines), electronics (tested for EMC compliance), agricultural products (Bureau of Plant Industry)

### Data Privacy (RA 10173)

The Data Privacy Act requires:
- Registration with National Privacy Commission (NPC) if you process personal data of Philippine residents and have 250+ employees or process sensitive personal information.
- Small foreign sellers: low enforcement risk, but data breach liability still applies.

### COD Trust Gap

Philippines has one of SEA's lowest online payment trust scores. Platform data suggests 50-60% of transactions are COD. Return-to-sender rates on failed COD can reach 25-30% in provincial areas. Mitigation:

1. Use marketplace-provided buyer rating to filter high-risk COD orders.
2. Offer small COD discount (PHP 20-50) for switching to digital payment.
3. Restrict COD for high-value items (> PHP 2,000) in the first 90 days.

## Malaysia

### Business-Friendly Baseline

Malaysia has the most straightforward regulatory environment for foreign e-commerce among the non-Singapore markets:

- **No foreign ownership restriction** on e-commerce (retail is in the Distributive Trade category, which is largely liberalized).
- SST (Sales and Service Tax) at 10% (sales) / 8% (services) applies; replaced GST in 2018.
- De minimis: MYR 500 (~USD 107) for low-value goods via postal/courier.

### Low-Value Goods (LVG) Tax

From 2024, the government extended LVG tax collection obligations:

- Goods ≤ MYR 500 imported via express courier or postal: 10% sales tax applied at point of sale.
- Shopee MY and Lazada MY are registered as LVG tax collectors and remit on seller's behalf.
- **Action required**: Confirm with marketplace that they are collecting and remitting LVG tax; do not double-collect.

### Halal Certification

Malaysia is 60%+ Muslim. For food, beverages, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals:

- **Halal certification from JAKIM** (Department of Islamic Development Malaysia) is not legally mandatory but is a strong commercial requirement.
- Products without halal cert face significant market limitation; Shopee MY may restrict certain food listings.
- Application via myhalal.gov.my; overseas manufacturers can apply but must have local representative.

### PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act)

- Applies to personal data of Malaysian residents processed in Malaysia.
- As a foreign seller using Malaysian marketplace: data processing is primarily the marketplace's responsibility; however, if you collect data via own site, PDPA applies.
- No registration required (unlike some countries); compliance is behavioral.

## Singapore

### Cleanest Regulatory Environment

Singapore requires no local entity for cross-border e-commerce. Key facts:

- GST (Goods and Services Tax): 9% as of 2024.
- **Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR)**: Foreign businesses with > SGD 100K in B2C digital sales to Singapore must register for GST. Physical goods imported under SGD 400 per shipment are de minimis (no GST). Goods over SGD 400 attract GST + applicable import duty.
- From Jan 2023: Low-value goods (≤ SGD 400) imported via air/post now subject to GST via OVR scheme; marketplaces collect on behalf of sellers.

### Product Safety

Singapore's Consumer Protection (Safety Requirements) Regulations require:

- **SAFETY mark** for 45 categories of controlled goods (electronics, electrical appliances, etc.) before sale.
- Administered by Singapore Accreditation Council (SAC) and Spring Singapore.
- Non-compliance: fine up to SGD 10,000 or imprisonment.

### PDPA Singapore

- PDPC (Personal Data Protection Commission) enforces.
- Mandatory breach notification within 3 days for significant breaches.
- Financial penalty up to SGD 1M or 10% of annual turnover (whichever higher) for larger organizations.
- Small foreign sellers: practical risk is low, but data handling must be lawful.

## Cross-Border Regulatory Decision Framework

Use this when advising on whether to sell cross-border vs. establish local entity:

```
Step 1: Check product category
  → Requires local permit (food, cosmetics, electronics)?
    YES → Must either obtain permit or sell through licensed local distributor
    NO  → Continue to Step 2

Step 2: Check annual revenue threshold
  → Expected year-1 revenue per country > USD 50K equivalent?
    YES → Evaluate local entity (tax registration likely required)
    NO  → Cross-border via marketplace program is acceptable to start

Step 3: Check platform cross-border program availability
  → Does target platform (Shopee/Lazada) have a cross-border seller program for your country?
    YES → Enroll; platform handles customs clearance + tax remittance
    NO  → Need freight forwarder + customs broker; higher operational overhead

Step 4: Check de minimis vs. average order value (AOV)
  → Is AOV likely below de minimis threshold for the target country?
    YES → Low customs friction; prioritize cross-border
    NO  → Duty + VAT will inflate landed cost; model impact on price competitiveness
```

### De Minimis Quick Reference

| Country | De Minimis | Currency | Approx. USD |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Indonesia | IDR 45,000 | IDR | ~USD 3 |
| Vietnam | None (all dutiable) | — | USD 0 |
| Thailand | THB 1,500 | THB | ~USD 42 |
| Philippines | PHP 10,000 | PHP | ~USD 175 |
| Malaysia | MYR 500 | MYR | ~USD 107 |
| Singapore | SGD 400 | SGD | ~USD 295 |

Vietnam's zero de minimis and Indonesia's USD 3 threshold mean cross-border shipments from Taiwan are almost always subject to import duty. Budget accordingly or use in-country fulfillment.

## Common Regulatory Gotchas

**Gotcha 1 — Platform Cross-Border Programs ≠ Full Legal Compliance**
Enrolling in Shopee's cross-border seller program handles logistics and basic customs clearance. It does NOT absolve you of product compliance obligations (BPOM, FDA, SNI, etc.). A product without required local certification can be seized at customs even in a "compliant" cross-border shipment.

**Gotcha 2 — Vietnam FCT Withholding Is on Gross Revenue, Not Profit**
The 1% FCT on Vietnam-sourced revenue is calculated on your gross sales, not your margin. On a 20% gross margin product, this represents a 5% hit on profit before any other costs. Model this explicitly.

**Gotcha 3 — Indonesia PT PMA Capital Requirement Is Paid-In**
The IDR 10 billion minimum capital for PT PMA in retail is paid-in capital — actual cash deposited in an Indonesian bank account. It is not a bond or letter of credit. This is a real capital commitment that eliminates PT PMA as an option for most SMBs in early-stage expansion.

**Gotcha 4 — Halal Certification Lead Time in Malaysia**
JAKIM Halal certification takes 6-18 months. Launching food/cosmetics in Malaysia and "planning to get halal later" means operating in a restricted market for over a year. Either start the application before launch or accept the market limitation.

**Gotcha 5 — Singapore OVR Registration Threshold Is Per Business, Not Per Platform**
If your total digital sales to Singapore (across all platforms: Shopee SG + Lazada SG + your own site) exceed SGD 100K, you must register for GST regardless of how the revenue is distributed. The threshold is not per-channel.

**Gotcha 6 — Philippine FDA Registration Takes Longer Than Market Timelines**
FDA Philippines registration for food/supplements can take 12-24 months. Selling unregistered health products on Philippine marketplaces is technically illegal and products can be pulled. If your product requires FDA PH registration, account for this in your market entry timeline or enter with non-regulated product SKUs first.
