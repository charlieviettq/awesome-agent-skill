# SEA Market Entry: Country Regulatory Quick-Reference

Covers: Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore.
Focus: foreign investment rules, e-commerce registration, import duties, tax obligations, data localization.
Last updated basis: 2024–2025 regulations. **Always verify with local counsel before committing capital** — SEA governments revise these rules frequently.

---

## How to Use This Reference

1. Identify your **entry mode** from the parent skill (marketplace / distributor / subsidiary).
2. Look up the target country section below.

## Decision: Entity or Not?

Before diving into country specifics, determine if you need a local legal entity at all.

| Entry Mode | Local Entity Required? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Shopee/Lazada cross-border store | No | Seller account is enough; customs handled by marketplace |
| Shopee/Lazada local store | Yes (or partner) | Requires local business registration or local partner |
| Own website, shipping from Taiwan | No | But tax obligations in destination country may still apply |
| Distributor/agent model | No (distributor holds entity) | You contract with a local company that handles local compliance |
| Joint venture | Yes | JV requires registered entity |
| WFOE / Wholly-owned subsidiary | Yes | Full local incorporation |

**Rule of thumb**: If your first-year revenue target is under USD 100K and you're using a marketplace, avoid incorporating locally. Incorporate when you need: (a) a local bank account for settlements, (b) to hire local employees, (c) to import in your own name, or (d) when your distributor requires it contractually.

## Indonesia

### Foreign Ownership (Negative List)

Indonesia uses a **Positive Investment List** (Daftar Prioritas Investasi, effective 2021). Most sectors are open to 100% foreign ownership if they're on the priority list, but e-commerce and retail have specific caps.

| Sector | Max Foreign Ownership | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Retail trade (physical) | 67% | Minimum IDR 10B capital per outlet |
| E-commerce platform | 49% | Applies to platforms; sellers are not platforms |
| Wholesale trade | 100% | Often used for B2B distribution |
| Logistics / warehousing | 100% | |
| Food & beverage manufacturing | 100% | |

**Practical path for Taiwan SMEs**: Register a PT PMA (Perseroan Terbatas Penanaman Modal Asing — foreign-owned limited liability company). Minimum paid-up capital IDR 10 billion (~USD 640K) for most trading activities. Alternative: use a **local nominee distributor** (Indonesian PT) and avoid incorporation until you validate the market.

### Import / Customs

| Tax Type | Rate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Import duty (MFN) | 0–40%; 5–15% for most consumer goods | HS code-dependent; check BTKI tariff schedule |
| VAT on import | 11% | On CIF + import duty |
| Income tax on import (PPh 22) | 2.5% (importer with API) or 7.5% (without API) | API = Angka Pengenal Importir, the importer registration number |
| Luxury goods tax (PPnBM) | 10–200% | Applies to cosmetics, electronics, certain categories |

**API Registration**: Without an API (Angka Pengenal Importir), your goods clear at 7.5% income tax on import vs. 2.5%. If you're shipping regular volume, API registration through a local entity or a licensed customs broker (PPJK) pays off quickly.

**De minimis threshold**: IDR 1,500,000 (~USD 95) CIF value per shipment is exempt from import duty and VAT. Shopee/Lazada cross-border uses this heavily for B2C parcels.

### Tax Obligations

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (PPh Badan) | 22% | 11% rate if company lists 40%+ on IDX |
| VAT (PPN) | 11% | Registration mandatory once annual turnover > IDR 4.8B (~USD 305K) |
| Withholding on services paid to non-residents | 20% (or treaty rate) | Taiwan-Indonesia DTA: 10% on royalties, 10-15% on dividends |
| Personal income tax (if employing locals) | Progressive 5–35% | |

**Taiwan-Indonesia Double Tax Agreement (DTA)**: In force. Withholding tax on dividends reduced to 10% (≥25% shareholding) or 15%. On interest: 10%. On royalties: 10%.

### Data Localization

**Government Regulation 71/2019 (GR 71)** and the **Personal Data Protection Law (UU PDP, enacted 2022, enforcement 2024)**:

- Electronic System Operators (ESO) that process "strategic" data must store and process within Indonesian territory.
- "Strategic" = financial, health, defense, energy, education data.
- Consumer e-commerce data: can be stored overseas but must be accessible to Indonesian authorities on demand.
- Practical minimum: appoint a local Data Protection Officer if you have >250 employees OR process sensitive data categories.

## Vietnam

### Foreign Ownership

Vietnam uses a **conditional sector list** under the Law on Investment 2020. Key for cross-border commerce:

| Sector | Foreign Ownership Cap | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Retail distribution (multi-outlet) | 100% | Subject to Economic Needs Test (ENT) for outlets > 500m² or beyond first outlet |
| E-commerce (platform) | 49% per WTO schedule | Applies to platforms, not individual sellers |
| E-commerce (seller/merchant) | 100% via trading company | Register as a trading company (công ty TNHH / cổ phần) |
| Manufacturing | 100% most categories | |

**Economic Needs Test (ENT)**: If you open more than one physical retail outlet in Vietnam, the provincial authority conducts an ENT before approving the license. No fixed criteria — highly discretionary. Avoid brick-and-mortar expansion before proving online demand.

**Minimum charter capital**: No statutory minimum for most trading companies, but VND 300M (~USD 12K) is a common practical floor for opening a bank account and passing credibility checks.

### Import / Customs

| Tax | Rate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Import duty (MFN) | Varies; 0–30% consumer goods | Check AHTN tariff schedule; ASEAN ATIGA rate often 0-5% for intra-ASEAN |
| VAT on import | 10% (or 5% for essential goods) | |
| Special consumption tax | 15–150% | Luxury cars, alcohol, tobacco, some electronics |

**CPTPP advantage**: Taiwan is not in CPTPP; goods shipped from Taiwan get MFN rates. However, goods manufactured in Vietnam (via FDI subsidiary or contract manufacturer) can enjoy CPTPP preferential rates to Japan, Canada, Australia, etc.

**De minimis**: VND 1,000,000 (~USD 40) CIF per consignment; below this, no import duty, no VAT for B2C e-commerce parcels.

### Tax Obligations

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (CIT) | 20% standard | 10% for high-tech, 17% for small enterprises |
| VAT | 10% / 5% / 0% | Mandatory registration once turnover > VND 1B/year (~USD 40K) |
| Withholding tax (FCT) for foreign suppliers | 10% VAT component + 5% CIT component | Applies when Vietnamese company pays service fees abroad |
| Personal income tax | Progressive 5–35% | |

**Foreign Contractor Tax (FCT)** is the major gotcha: if a Vietnamese company pays a foreign (e.g., Taiwan) company for services, the Vietnamese payer must withhold FCT (effectively 5% on net revenue for most services + 10% VAT). Structure contracts carefully to allocate which services are performed "in Vietnam" vs. "offshore."

**Taiwan-Vietnam DTA**: In force. Withholding on dividends: 15% (general) / 10% (≥70% shareholding); on royalties: 15%; on interest: 10%.

### Data Localization

**Cybersecurity Law (Law 24/2018)** + **Decree 13/2023** (Personal Data Protection):

- Domestic data storage required for: national security-related data, personal data of Vietnamese users collected by platforms with >10,000 users/year.
- Practical interpretation for most e-commerce merchants: store transaction data locally OR ensure instant access/portability to Vietnamese authorities within 24 hours of request.
- Appoint a local representative if you offer services targeting Vietnamese users and process their personal data.

## Thailand

### Foreign Ownership

**Foreign Business Act B.E. 2542 (1999)** categorizes restricted businesses in three lists:

| List | Description | Max Foreign Ownership |
|---|---|---|
| List 1 | Reserved for Thais (media, land, etc.) | 0% |
| List 2 | Requires cabinet approval | 40-60%, case by case |
| List 3 | Requires Foreign Business License (FBL) | 49% default; FBL can allow more |

**Retail and wholesale trade** fall under List 3 — foreigners can hold up to 49% without FBL, or can apply for FBL or use Board of Investment (BOI) promotion.

**BOI promotion**: If your business qualifies (tech, R&D, high-value manufacturing), BOI can grant 100% foreign ownership exemption from the FBA + tax holidays (3-8 years CIT exemption). Applications take 3-6 months.

**Treaty of Amity (USA only)**: Only US citizens get 100% ownership in most sectors — this does NOT apply to Taiwanese companies.

**Practical path**: Use a **Thai registered company** with a local partner holding 51% (common, but be careful about nominee structures — illegal under Thai law). Alternatively, BOI for eligible sectors.

### Import / Customs

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Import duty | 0-30%; ~10-20% typical consumer goods | ASEAN ATIGA: 0% for ASEAN-origin goods |
| VAT on import | 7% | Temporarily reduced from 10%; review annually |
| Excise tax | Category-specific | Alcohol, tobacco, luxury watches, cars |

**De minimis**: THB 1,500 (~USD 42) CIF per consignment; below this, no duty or VAT for B2C parcels.

**Taiwan-Thailand trade**: Taiwan is not ASEAN; goods from Taiwan do not qualify for ATIGA zero rates. Effective duty on most consumer products from Taiwan: MFN rate (typically 10-20%).

### Tax Obligations

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax | 20% | 15% for SMEs on profit 300K-3M THB |
| VAT | 7% (temporarily) | Registration mandatory once annual revenue > THB 1.8M (~USD 51K) |
| Withholding tax on dividends (non-resident) | 10% | |
| Withholding tax on royalties (non-resident) | 15% | Taiwan-Thailand DTA: reduces to 10-15% |
| Specific Business Tax (SBT) | 3.3% | On financial services, real estate; not typical e-commerce |

**Taiwan-Thailand DTA**: In force. Dividend WHT: 10%; Royalty WHT: 10-15%; Interest WHT: 10-15%.

### Data Localization

**Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), effective 2022**:

- No mandatory data localization — data can be stored abroad.
- Cross-border data transfer allowed if recipient country has "adequate" protection standard OR specific safeguards (standard contractual clauses, binding corporate rules) are in place.
- Must have a Privacy Policy in Thai; must honor data subject rights (access, deletion, portability).
- Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) if processing sensitive data at scale.

## Malaysia

### Foreign Ownership

Malaysia is the most **foreign-investment friendly** of the major SEA markets for e-commerce:

- 100% foreign ownership allowed for most manufacturing and services sectors.
- Retail: Foreign-owned retail companies can operate if they meet one of: (a) minimum paid-up capital MYR 5M (~USD 1.1M), or (b) product is unique/not available locally.
- E-commerce companies: **100% foreign ownership allowed** — no FBA equivalent.

**Entity types**: Sdn. Bhd. (private limited) most common. Setup time: 1-2 weeks via SSM (Companies Commission). Minimum one resident director required.

### Import / Customs

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Import duty | 0-30%; 5-15% typical | ASEAN ATIGA 0% for qualifying ASEAN origin |
| Sales & Service Tax (SST) — sales tax | 5% or 10% | Replaced GST in 2018; applies to manufacturers and importers |
| SST — service tax | 8% | Applies to taxable services |

**De minimis**: MYR 500 (~USD 106) CIF; B2C parcels below this are duty-free.

**Low-Value Goods (LVG) Tax**: From Jan 2024, all imported goods sold online ≤MYR 500 are subject to 10% LVG tax collected by the e-commerce platform. Shopee/Lazada handle this automatically for cross-border transactions.

### Tax Obligations

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax | 24% | 17% for first MYR 600K profit for SMEs |
| SST registration | Mandatory once sales > MYR 500K/year (~USD 106K) | |
| Withholding tax (dividends, non-resident) | 0% | Single-tier dividend system — no WHT on dividends |
| Withholding tax (royalties) | 10% | Taiwan-Malaysia DTA: 10% |
| Withholding tax (interest) | 15% | Taiwan-Malaysia DTA: 10-15% |

**Taiwan-Malaysia DTA**: In force.

### Data Localization

**Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA 2010)**, amended 2024:

- No data localization requirement.
- Cross-border data transfer to countries with adequate protection is allowed; whitelist published by regulator.
- Must register with the Personal Data Protection Commissioner if processing personal data in Malaysia (registration fee MYR 200-1,000 depending on sector).

## Philippines

### Foreign Ownership

**Republic Act 11647 (2022 FIA Amendment)** liberalized several sectors:

| Sector | Old Limit | New Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Retail trade | 60% foreign OK if >USD 2.5M capital | 100% allowed if >USD 2.5M paid-up |
| E-commerce | Was ambiguous | Treated as retail/service; 100% now allowed above capital threshold |
| Public services (utilities, telecoms) | 40% cap | 100% now allowed except: aviation, shipping, mass media |

**Minimum capital for 100% foreign retail**: USD 2,500,000 paid-up capital. This is prohibitive for most SMEs — consider distributor model instead.

**SEC registration**: Required for doing business. Timeline: 4-8 weeks. Requires at least one Filipino resident officer.

### Import / Customs

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Import duty | 0-30%; CMTA tariff schedule | ASEAN ATIGA 0% for ASEAN origin |
| VAT on import | 12% | |
| Excise tax | Category-specific | Alcohol, tobacco, petroleum |

**De minimis**: PHP 10,000 (~USD 175) CIF; below this, no customs duty. Note: VAT still applies to imports above PHP 10,000 value (RA 10863 / CMTA). There is currently an active push in Congress to lower de minimis for e-commerce — monitor this.

### Tax Obligations

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax (RCIT) | 25% | 20% for net taxable income ≤ PHP 5M AND total assets ≤ PHP 100M |
| VAT | 12% | Mandatory registration once gross sales > PHP 3M/year (~USD 52K) |
| Withholding tax on dividends (non-resident) | 25% (15% if country gives credit for difference) | |
| Withholding tax on royalties | 25% (10% if qualified) | |

**No Taiwan-Philippines DTA**: Taiwan and Philippines have no tax treaty. Standard withholding rates apply. This is the highest WHT exposure among covered countries — factor it into distributor contract structures.

### Data Localization

**Republic Act 10173 (Data Privacy Act 2012)** + NPC regulations:

- No mandatory data localization.
- Cross-border data transfer allowed with contractual safeguards (DPA between transferor and transferee).
- Must register Data Processing Systems with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) if you have >250 employees or process sensitive personal information.

## Singapore

### Foreign Ownership

**100% foreign ownership** allowed in virtually all sectors. No FDI approval process. Singapore is the natural **regional headquarters** (RHQ) choice for SEA expansion.

**Entity setup**: Private Limited (Pte. Ltd.) — fastest in SEA. Online registration via ACRA BizFile+: 1-3 business days. Minimum 1 resident director (Singapore citizen, PR, or EP holder).

**Singapore as RHQ strategy**: Incorporate in Singapore → invoice/manage other SEA subsidiaries from Singapore → benefit from Singapore's 80+ DTA network, IP box regime (5% CIT on IP income), and Global Trader Programme.

### Import / Customs

Singapore has **zero import duty** on almost all goods. Exceptions: tobacco, petroleum, intoxicating liquors, motor vehicles.

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| GST on import | 9% (from Jan 2024) | Applies to all goods; previously B2C imports <SGD 400 were exempt |
| Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR) | 9% GST | Non-resident suppliers with >SGD 1M global turnover + >SGD 100K Singapore sales must register and charge GST |

### Tax Obligations

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income tax | 17% | Effective rate much lower with startup exemptions |
| Startup tax exemption | 75% exempt first SGD 100K + 50% on next SGD 100K | First 3 consecutive YAs |
| GST | 9% | Register if annual taxable turnover > SGD 1M |
| Withholding tax on dividends | 0% | One-tier dividend system |
| Withholding tax on royalties (non-resident) | 10% | |

**Taiwan-Singapore DTA**: In force. Competitive withholding rates.

### Data Localization

**Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) 2012**, amended 2020:

- No data localization requirement — among the most permissive in ASEAN.
- Cross-border transfer allowed if recipient country has comparable protection OR specific exceptions apply.
- Mandatory data breach notification to PDPC within 3 business days if breach affects >500 individuals or causes significant harm.

## Regulatory Monitoring Checklist

Changes happen fast. Build this into operations from Day 1:

**Monthly**
- [ ] Check official gazette/government website for new e-commerce regulations in target country
- [ ] Monitor customs authority announcements for tariff changes to your HS codes
- [ ] Review marketplace (Shopee/Lazada) seller center announcements — they often translate regulatory changes into seller requirements

**Quarterly**
- [ ] Review VAT/GST threshold — if you're approaching registration threshold, engage local accountant
- [ ] Check for new DTA developments (Taiwan actively pursues new agreements)
- [ ] Review de minimis thresholds — Philippines and Vietnam have active proposals to change them

**Annually**
- [ ] Full regulatory audit with local counsel in each operating country
- [ ] Review foreign ownership rules — liberalization moves quickly (Philippines 2022 is a good example)
- [ ] Review data protection compliance — enforcement is increasing across all countries

## Taiwan-Specific Advantages to Leverage

1. **DTA network**: Taiwan has DTAs with Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand, Malaysia, Philippines, and Singapore — use them to reduce withholding tax drag on inter-company payments.
2. **TAITRA / MOEA support**: Taiwan's trade promotion agencies offer subsidies for market entry research and trade show participation in SEA. Check taitra.org.tw for current programs.
3. **"Made in Taiwan" origin labeling**: For manufactured goods, Taiwan-origin certification from TAITRA can be a marketing asset. Verify rules of origin for each country's tariff preference claims.
4. **Taiwan-ASEAN investment protection agreements**: Taiwan has separate Investment Protection Agreements (IPAs) with several ASEAN members — these provide arbitration access even without formal diplomatic relations.

## When to Engage Local Counsel (Not Optional)

Do NOT self-navigate these situations — engage a licensed local attorney:

- Any physical retail presence in Vietnam (ENT triggers)
- Thailand BOI application
- Philippines SEC registration and capital requirements
- Indonesia PT PMA incorporation and API registration
- Any M&A or JV negotiation in any SEA country
- Data breach response in any country where you hold local user data
- Any customs dispute or import license application
