# International Relations Glossary & Sensitive Terminology

## Diplomacy & Treaties

| Term | Definition | Usage Note |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Bilateral** | Between two countries | e.g., "bilateral trade agreement" |
| **Multilateral** | Among three or more countries/parties | e.g., "multilateral climate accord" |
| **Treaty** | Formal, binding international agreement, ratified by legislatures | Legally enforceable; breach can trigger dispute mechanisms |
| **Convention** | Formal agreement (synonym for treaty in legal context) | Same legal weight as treaty |
| **Accord** | Broader term; may be formal (binding) or non-binding | Check context; "accord" alone doesn't guarantee legal force |
| **MOU (Memorandum of Understanding)** | Intent to cooperate; typically non-binding | Often precedes formal treaty; weaker commitment |
| **Protocol** | Amendment or supplementary agreement to a treaty | e.g., "Kyoto Protocol" (amendment to UNFCCC) |
| **Covenant** | Formal agreement, often emphasizing principles | e.g., "International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights" (binding) |
| **Demarche** | Formal diplomatic protest (official, on-the-record) | Step below breaking relations; signals serious displeasure |
| **Aide-mémoire** | Informal diplomatic record (not official note, less formal) | Used for negotiations, preliminary talks |
| **Formal note** | Official diplomatic communication, on record with foreign ministry | More formal than aide-mémoire; can be published |
| **Background briefing** | Off-the-record or not-for-attribution diplomatic explanation | Source: typically US State Dept practice; risky for journalists to rely on without corroboration |

## Cross-Strait / Taiwan-Specific Terminology

| Chinese Term | English Options | Political Load | Usage Guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **中國** | China, PRC, Chinese mainland | Neutral when referring to People's Republic of China | Standard usage for PRC |
| **中華人民共和國** | People's Republic of China (formal) | Neutral; official name | Use in formal/legal contexts |
| **大陸** | Mainland (euphemistic); the Continent | Common in Taiwan media; avoids "China" | Less politically charged; common in cross-strait coverage |
| **中共** | CCP, Chinese Communist Party | Charged; suggests regime critique | Avoid unless specifically referring to the party; "PRC government" is clearer |
| **台灣** | Taiwan | Neutral | Standard usage; least controversial |
| **中華民國** | Republic of China (formal ROC) | Politically loaded; signals non-independence position | Use only when quoting official ROC designation or in historical context |
| **島嶼** | Island, territory | Descriptive, avoids sovereignty claims | Useful when sovereignty is contested in the piece |
| **政府** | Government | Neutral | Standard for any government, including ROC/Taiwan |
| **當局** | Authorities; the authorities (implies non-legitimacy) | Somewhat charged; suggests writer's skepticism | Avoid unless specifically contrasting with legitimate government |
| **政權** | Regime | Very charged; implies authoritarian non-legitimacy | Reserve for autocracies; do not use for democracies |
| **九二共識** | 1992 Consensus | Political; disputed definition | Note that different parties define it differently; do not present as settled fact |

## Conflict Terminology (Legal Precision Required)

| Term | Legal Definition | Journalistic Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **War crime** | Violation of laws of war (Geneva Conventions, Protocols) | Use only: "accused of" or "alleged war crime" until adjudicated by tribunal |
| **Crime against humanity** | Systematic attack on civilian population | Use: "alleged crime against humanity" or "accused of crimes against humanity" |
| **Genocide** | Intentional destruction of ethnic/national/religious/racial group | Highest legal bar; ICC jurisdiction; use "alleged genocide" until tribunal judgment |
| **Terrorism / Terrorist** | Varies by jurisdiction (US list ≠ EU list ≠ UN list) | State whose list: "designated terrorist organization by [X country]." Do not use "terrorist" as descriptor without attribution |
| **Militant** | Armed non-state actor | More neutral than terrorist; may refer to insurgents, guerrillas, fighters |
| **Insurgent / Insurgency** | Non-state armed group challenging state authority | Neutral descriptor; standard military term |
| **Occupation** | Military control of territory of another state | Legally loaded; "occupation" per international law has specific meaning (Hague Regulations, 1949 Geneva Convention IV). Use "occupation" only if territory is clearly not the occupier's sovereign territory. |
| **Disputed territory** | Territory claimed by 2+ parties | More neutral when sovereignty is contested (e.g., Cyprus, Kashmir) |
| **Controlled by** | De facto military/administrative control | Most neutral: "territory controlled by X" avoids sovereignty claims |
| **Regime** | Government system (often implying authoritarian) | Use sparingly; "government" is clearer and less charged |
| **Government** | Sovereign political authority | Neutral; standard usage |

## Humanitarian & Rights Terminology

| Term | Definition | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **IDP (Internally Displaced Person)** | Person displaced within their own country | Does not cross international border; specific legal status under UN protocol |
| **Refugee** | Person displaced across international border due to persecution or conflict | Specific legal status; triggering international obligation to provide asylum |
| **Asylum seeker** | Person claiming refugee status, awaiting determination | Status pending; do not conflate with refugee (have not yet been granted status) |
| **Migrant** | Person moving for economic or other non-persecution reasons | Distinct from refugee; different legal protections |
| **Humanitarian corridor** | Safe passage for humanitarian supplies and sometimes evacuees | May be negotiated between parties; not a guarantee of neutrality |
| **Humanitarian access** | Ability of aid organizations to reach affected populations | Often restricted in conflict zones; limiting access is serious concern |
| **Neutrality (humanitarian principle)** | Impartiality in aid delivery; ICRC concept | Not political neutrality; ICRC can criticize actions while remaining operationally neutral |
| **Sanction / Sanctions regime** | Government-imposed economic/political punishment | Specific term with legal meaning; "asset freeze," "embargo," "restrictive measures" have distinct scope |
| **Embargo** | Comprehensive ban on trade (often oil/arms) | Stricter than targeted sanctions |
| **Asset freeze** | Blocking of financial assets | Narrower than full sanctions |

## Regional / Historical Terms

| Term | Context | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Rohingya** | Myanmar ethnic group; refugee population | Carefully verify nationality claims; historically contested |
| **Kurds** | Ethnic group spanning Turkey, Iraq, Iran, Syria | Distinct national aspirations; use "Kurdish-majority region" not "Kurdistan" unless quoting official designation |
| **Kashmir** | Disputed territory between India and Pakistan | Avoid "Indian-administered Kashmir" vs "Pakistan-administered Kashmir"; use "territory controlled by…" with context |
| **Crimea** | Peninsula annexed by Russia in 2014 | International consensus: Russia annexed; some countries recognize, most do not. Use "Russian-annexed Crimea" or "territory Russia claims / territories Russia controls" |
| **Golan Heights** | Territory Israel captured from Syria in 1967 | Disputed; Israel calls it Israeli territory, Syria calls it occupied Syrian territory. Use "Israeli-controlled Golan Heights" + context |
| **Taiwan** | Sovereign state / province per PRC / separate country per US / people's democracy per Taiwan government | Clarity required: specify which actor's designation you are citing |
| **Orthodox Church** / **Russian Orthodox** | Christian denomination; often geopolitically significant in Eastern Europe | Note: Putin uses ROC as tool for soft power in Near Abroad |

## Sanctions & Trade Terminology

| Term | Definition | Distinction |
|---|---|---|
| **Sanction** | Broad term for punitive measure | Can be military, economic, diplomatic |
| **Economic sanction** | Trade/financial restriction | Most common type; varies in scope (sector-specific vs. comprehensive) |
| **Targeted sanction** | Directed at individuals, companies, sectors | Also called "smart sanctions" (attempt to avoid civilian harm) |
| **Secondary sanction** | Punishment for not complying with primary sanction (e.g., US sanctioning foreign companies trading with Iran) | Controversial; extraterritorial application |
| **Counter-sanction** | Reciprocal sanction in response | e.g., Russia sanctioning US officials in response to US sanctions on Russia |
| **Restrictive measures** | EU term for what US calls "sanctions" | Same concept; different terminology by jurisdiction |
| **OFAC list** | US Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control designations | Specific, published list; cite it: "designated by OFAC" |
| **EU sanctions list** | EU Council decisions on restrictive measures | Distinct from US list; note differences in coverage |

## Key Phrase Alerts (Politically Loaded)

- "Western countries" — Vague; specify: "NATO countries," "G7," "US and European allies," etc.
- "International community" — Often code for "Western consensus"; specify actual coalition.
- "Rogue state" — Charged language; use only in direct quote or with attribution: "US-designated rogue state" or avoid entirely.
- "Terrorist haven" — Charged; use "host to designated terrorist organizations" + specifics.
- "Ethnic cleansing" — Not legal term (unlike genocide/crimes against humanity); use "forced displacement" or cite sources saying "ethnic cleansing."
- "Democratic backsliding" — Evaluative; "restrictions on democratic institutions" is more neutral.
- "Failed state" — Fragile state; more neutral term available.

## Non-English Language Sourcing Cautions

When translating official statements, watch for:

1. **"政府" (Chinese govt)** vs **"當局"** — Subtle but critical shift in legitimacy framing.
2. **"佔領" (Chinese, occupation)** vs **"控制" (control)** — Former is more politically charged.
3. **Russian "миротворец" (peacekeeper)** vs **"наемник" (mercenary)** — Same actor, vastly different frame depending on language used in original.
4. **Arabic "نكبة" (nakba, catastrophe)** vs **"النزوح" (displacement)** — Historical/political weight in Arabic version not captured in English translation.

Always source-check politically fraught translations with native speakers before publication.
