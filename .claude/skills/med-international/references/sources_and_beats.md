# International News Sources and Beats

## Primary Official Sources

### United Nations System
- **OCHA (Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs)**: situation reports, emergency appeals, humanitarian snapshots. Site: reliefweb.int
- **UNHCR (Refugee Agency)**: refugee/IDP statistics, country reports, protection standards. Site: unhcr.org
- **UNESCO, WHO, UNDP, FAO, UNICEF**: sector-specific reports (health, education, food security, development).
- **UN Security Council**: official press releases, resolutions (always cite vote count + abstentions).
- **UN General Assembly**: resolutions, voting records available on un.org

### International Financial Institutions
- **IMF (International Monetary Fund)**: economic surveillance, Country Reports, balance-of-payments data.
- **World Bank**: development projects, country briefs, data repositories.
- **OECD**: stats on development, governance, trade.
- **Asian Development Bank / African Development Bank / Inter-American DB**: regional funding and policy.

### International Organizations
- **WHO (World Health Organization)**: disease surveillance, health emergencies, technical guidelines.
- **ICRC (International Committee of the Red Cross)**: conflict-zone humanitarian access, detention monitoring, IHL enforcement.
- **INTERPOL**: international crime alerts (though note: alerts do not imply guilt).
- **IAEA (International Atomic Energy Agency)**: nuclear monitoring, safeguards compliance.

### Government Foreign Ministries
- **US State Department**: daily press briefings, country reports, sanctions lists (OFAC).
- **EU EEAS (European External Action Service)**: statements, sanctions, diplomatic positions.
- **MOFA (Taiwan Ministry of Foreign Affairs)**: bilaterial positions, regional statements.
- **MOFA (Japan), Foreign Ministry (South Korea, Australia)**: regional statements, alliance positions.
- **Russia MFA, China MOFA**: note: statements must be cross-checked with third-party verification due to information-control context.

### International Courts & Tribunals
- **International Criminal Court (ICC)**: indictments, trial decisions, prosecutor statements. Site: icc-cpi.int
- **International Court of Justice**: advisory opinions, case decisions.
- **Regional human-rights courts**: European Court of Human Rights, Inter-American Court of Human Rights, African Court.

## News Wire Agencies (Primary-Tier International Reporting)
- **Reuters**: global reach, real-time reporting; generally attribute sources transparently.
- **Associated Press (AP)**: US-centered but global network; professional standards well-established.
- **Agence France-Presse (AFP)**: French-language strength; good coverage of francophone Africa, Middle East.
- **BBC News**: UK public broadcaster; editorial standards available online.
- **Deutsche Welle (DW)**: German broadcaster with multilingual services; strong on Eastern Europe, Africa.
- **Radio Free Europe / Radio Liberty (RFE/RL)**: US government-funded but editorially independent; strong on Eastern Europe, Central Asia, Russia coverage.

## Regional and Diaspora Media (Secondary/Advocacy Tier)
- **Asia**: Nikkei Asia, South China Morning Post, The Hindu, Straits Times.
- **Middle East/North Africa**: Al Jazeera English, Middle East Eye, The National (UAE).
- **Africa**: Africa News (France 24 subsidiary), The African (pan-African), individual country outlets.
- **Europe**: Politico, The Guardian, Financial Times.
- **Diaspora outlets**: Note affiliation transparently. Example: "outlet staffed by exiles from the region, known to support opposition groups."

---

## NGO/Watchdog Sources (Advocacy Tier; Disclose Affiliation)
- **Human Rights**: Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch (both have regional biases; note them).
- **Humanitarian**: ICRC (technically intergovernmental), Médecins Sans Frontières, Oxfam, World Vision.
- **Conflict/War Documentation**: Bellingcat (open-source investigation), Syria Human Rights Observatory (opposition-affiliated; note).
- **Sanctions & Finance**: Transparency International, Financial Action Task Force.
- **Note**: NGO reports are valuable but must be labeled as advocacy sources, not independent fact.

## Journalist Safety and Fixer Networks

### Fixer Vetting
- **Background**: Where is fixer from? Which community? Political affiliation (if any)? Professional experience?
- **Disclosure standard**: If fixer's background shapes reporting perspective (e.g., fixer is from one side of ethnic divide), this must be logged on editorial side; in published piece, use generic attribution ("local contact") without naming.
- **Safety protocol**: Does fixer face retaliation risk? If yes, anonymize fully; if minimal risk, consider on-record credit.

### Safety and Security Resources
- **Dart Center**: dartcenter.org — trauma-informed reporting, journalist safety.
- **CPJ (Committee to Protect Journalists)**: cpj.org/resources — safety tips, detained-journalist tracking.
- **RISC Training**: risctraining.org — professional journalist safety courses.
- **Local embassy security**: Brief local embassy before deployment; register presence.

### Insurance and Support
- **Lloyd's of London**: journalist safety insurance (check if your outlet carries it).
- **Rory Peck Trust**: rpc.org.uk — support for freelance journalists, memorial fund.
- **International Women's Media Foundation**: iwmf.org — female-journalist safety.

## Regional Briefing Sources (Bookmark These)

### China / Cross-Strait
- **Taiwan MOFA statements**: mofa.gov.tw
- **China MOFA statements**: fmprc.gov.cn (note: official position, cross-check against third-party sources)
- **PRC official media for tone**: Xinhua, People's Daily (state position)
- **Independent cross-strait analysis**: Taiwan's Council of Strategic Studies, Carnegie Endowment China program, MERICS (Berlin, Germany)

### Asia-Pacific
- **ASEAN Secretariat**: aseanregional.org — regional statements, agreements.
- **Quad statements**: US State Department, Japanese MOFA, Australian Department of Foreign Affairs, Indian MEA.
- **Pacific Island sources**: Pacific Islands Forum Secretariat, individual country governments (often smaller presence = harder access).

### European Union
- **EU EEAS statements**: eeas.europa.eu
- **Council of Europe**: human-rights reporting, anti-corruption monitoring.
- **Individual EU member states**: foreign ministries (Germany: auswaertiges-amt.de, France: diplomatie.gouv.fr).

### Middle East / North Africa
- **Arab League**: statement portal, but note: consensus is rare, statements often reflect only powerful members.
- **Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC)**: gcc.org — Gulf states' coordination.
- **Iran**: careful sourcing; IRNA is state media; cross-check via international sources.
- **Israel**: GPO (Government Press Office), IDF spokesperson; also check independent Israeli media (Haaretz, +972 Magazine).
- **Palestine**: PA press office (Ramallah), Hamas media (Gaza). Note political divisions; separate statements by authority.

### Africa
- **African Union**: au.int — statements, peacekeeping operations.
- **Individual regional bodies**: ECOWAS (West Africa), EACRF (East Africa), SADC (Southern Africa).
- **Conflict monitoring**: UN MONUSCO (DRC), UNAMID (Darfur), UNMISS (South Sudan) — mission reports provide ground-level data.

## Key Sourcing Principles

1. **Official first**: When available, cite government/official source directly, not via media relay.
2. **Wire agency as secondary**: Reuters/AP reporting of an official statement is acceptable, but name the original source.
3. **NGO as advocacy**: Always label. "NGO X (which supports…) reported…" signals the source's perspective.
4. **Diaspora as minority voice**: "Exiled community sources reported…" is honest; "sources in the region reported" obscures that the source is exile-based.
5. **Fixer background as editor-side knowledge**: Editors must know fixer affiliation; published piece may use generic attribution but editor has full context.
6. **Translation sourcing**: "Translated from official statement" > "translated from media report" > "roughly translated from social media post" (last one is very weak).
