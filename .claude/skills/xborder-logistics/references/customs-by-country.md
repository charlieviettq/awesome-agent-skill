# Customs By Country

Reference for Taiwan-based cross-border sellers. Covers de minimis thresholds, import duty rates, required documentation, restricted product categories, and known compliance traps — by destination market.

---

## Landed Cost Formula

Every per-order cost estimate must account for:

```
Landed Cost = Product Cost
            + International Shipping
            + Insurance (typically 0.5–1% of declared value)
            + Import Duty (Customs Value × Duty Rate)
            + Local VAT/GST ((Customs Value + Duty) × VAT Rate)
            + Customs Brokerage Fee (if applicable)
            + Last-Mile Delivery Fee (if overseas warehouse)
```

**Customs Value** is almost always CIF (Cost + Insurance + Freight) in Asia-Pacific markets and FOB in some US/EU contexts. When in doubt, use CIF — it's the conservative (higher) estimate.

**Example: $50 skincare product shipped from Taiwan to Malaysia**
```
Product cost:         $50.00
Shipping (DHL):       $12.00
Insurance:             $0.62  (0.5% × $62 CIF basis... actually 0.5% × $50)
CIF value:            $62.62
Import duty:           $0.00  (cosmetics, most under 0% if CIF < RM 500 ≈ $110)
GST:                   $3.76  (6% × $62.62)
Total landed cost:    $66.38
```

## De Minimis Thresholds by Country

Below the threshold: no import duty, often no VAT/GST.

| Country | Threshold | Currency | Notes |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Malaysia | RM 500 | MYR ≈ USD 110 | GST-exempt below threshold |
| Singapore | SGD 400 | SGD ≈ USD 300 | GST (9%) applies to ALL values since Jan 2023 |
| Thailand | THB 1,500 | THB ≈ USD 42 | VAT (7%) applies even below threshold |
| Indonesia | IDR 500,000 | IDR ≈ USD 30 | One of the lowest in SEA — most B2C orders are dutiable |
| Philippines | PHP 10,000 | PHP ≈ USD 175 | Duty-free below threshold; VAT may still apply |
| Vietnam | VND 1,000,000 | VND ≈ USD 40 | Informal enforcement; varies by courier |
| United States | USD 800 | USD | Highest globally; Section 321 applies |
| Japan | JPY 10,000 | JPY ≈ USD 65 | Personal import exemption for small packages |
| South Korea | USD 150 | USD | Reduced to $150 from $200 in 2023 |
| Australia | AUD 1,000 | AUD ≈ USD 650 | GST (10%) applies to ALL imports since Jul 2018 |
| Hong Kong | No duty | — | Zero tariff territory; collect VAT/duties on re-export only |
| Taiwan (imports) | TWD 2,000 | TWD ≈ USD 62 | Reference: what Taiwan charges incoming shipments |

**Critical**: Indonesia and Thailand have low thresholds — assume most B2C orders will incur some tax. Build this into your pricing before entering these markets.

## Country-Specific Requirements

### Malaysia

**Regulatory body**: Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| General goods | Commercial invoice + packing list + AWB |
| Electronics | SIRIM certification (mandatory for most powered devices) |
| Cosmetics | NPRA (National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency) registration if selling commercially |
| Food | Food Safety and Quality Division permit; halal certification strongly recommended |
| Textiles | Certificate of Origin (Form D under ASEAN FTA reduces duty) |

**SIRIM trap**: Consumer electronics — including power banks, chargers, smart devices — require SIRIM approval before import. Shipping without it leads to detention at customs. The certification process takes 3-6 months. This is not optional for volume sellers.

**Duty rate examples** (MFN rates, Taiwan as non-FTA partner):
- Electronics/consumer goods: 0–25%
- Apparel: 15–30%
- Cosmetics: 0–10%
- Toys: 0–30%

**FTA note**: Malaysia and Taiwan do NOT have a bilateral FTA. Taiwan sellers cannot claim ASEAN-origin preferential rates. Certificate of Origin from Taiwan only establishes origin, not preferential duty.

### Singapore

**Regulatory body**: Singapore Customs + IMDA (electronics) + HSA (health products)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| All imports | GST registration required for overseas vendors with >SGD 1M global revenue selling B2C (since Jan 2023) |
| Electronics | IMDA or PSB certification for equipment that may cause radio interference |
| Health supplements | HSA notification or registration |
| Food | SFA import permit; specific products need pre-import approval |

**GST change in 2023**: Previously, goods under SGD 400 entering via post/air were GST-exempt. That exemption was removed January 1, 2023. All B2C digital goods and low-value goods now require GST collection at checkout by overseas vendors. If you sell to Singapore B2C and your global revenue exceeds SGD 1M, you must register for GST with IRAS.

**Practical impact**: For small Taiwan sellers below the threshold, major marketplaces (Shopee, Lazada) handle GST collection on your behalf. For direct DTC sales, check your revenue against the SGD 1M threshold.

### Thailand

**Regulatory body**: Thai Customs Department + FDA Thailand (food/cosmetics)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Cosmetics | Thai FDA cosmetics notification (for commercial imports) |
| Food | Thai FDA import permit (Form อ.1) |
| Electronics | NBTC registration for wireless/telecom devices |
| All commercial | Form KorShorKor 99 customs declaration |

**VAT collection**: Thailand charges 7% VAT on all imports regardless of value, collected at customs. For direct mail, customs brokers typically handle this — but it adds $3-7 per small parcel and causes 1-3 day delays.

**Duty rates** for common Taiwan exports to Thailand:
- Electronics: 0–20% (FTA with ASEAN, but Taiwan not in ASEAN)
- Apparel/textiles: 30%
- Cosmetics/beauty: 20%
- Plastic goods: 10–20%

### Indonesia

**Regulatory body**: Directorate General of Customs and Excise (DJBC) + BPOM (food/cosmetics/pharma)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Cosmetics | BPOM registration mandatory — no exceptions for commercial sales |
| Food/supplements | BPOM import permit (ML registration for imported food) |
| Electronics | SDPPI certification (Kominfo ministry) for devices with wireless capability |
| Textiles | SNI certification for some categories |
| General goods | PIB (import declaration) for >IDR 500,000 |

**BPOM trap**: Indonesia is one of the strictest markets for cosmetics and food supplements. A product without BPOM registration will be seized at customs. The registration process takes 3-12 months and costs $500-2,000+ USD per SKU. For small sellers, marketplace fulfillment (Tokopedia/Shopee Indonesia) often handles registered products — but verify the brand/SKU is already registered.

**De minimis is low (IDR 500,000 ≈ USD 30)**: Almost every B2C order from Taiwan will be above the threshold. Expect import duty on most shipments.

**Import duty examples** (non-FTA, CIF basis):
- Consumer electronics: 0–15%
- Apparel: 15–25% + possible safeguard duties
- Cosmetics: 10–15%

### Philippines

**Regulatory body**: Bureau of Customs (BOC) + FDA Philippines (health/food/cosmetics)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Food supplements | FDA Philippines LTO (License to Operate) + CPR (Certificate of Product Registration) |
| Cosmetics | FDA notification |
| Electronics | NTC type approval for radio/telecom devices |
| All imports >PHP 10,000 | Formal entry (IEIRD form) + commercial invoice + packing list |

**VAT on imports**: 12% VAT applies to all dutiable imports.

**Import duty**: Philippines uses AHTN tariff codes. MFN rates apply for Taiwan (not ASEAN FTA).
- Electronics: 0–15%
- Apparel: 10–30%
- Cosmetics: 0–10%

**De minimis change alert**: The PHP 10,000 threshold was raised from PHP 3,000 in 2022. As of 2024 this is confirmed, but the Philippines Customs has proposed further changes — verify the current threshold before pricing.

### United States

**Regulatory body**: CBP (Customs and Border Protection) + FDA + FCC

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| All imports <$800 | Section 321 informal entry — no formal customs declaration needed |
| Electronics | FCC ID required for all wireless/radio-frequency devices |
| Food | FDA Prior Notice required before every shipment |
| Supplements/vitamins | FDA requires accurate labeling + facility registration |
| Cosmetics | FDA labeling requirements (ingredient list, net weight in US units) |

**Section 321 (De Minimis)**: The USD 800 threshold is the world's most generous. Each unique person can receive one Section 321 shipment per day. For direct mail B2C, most Taiwan sellers will fall below this threshold and avoid formal entry entirely.

**Section 301 Tariffs**: Additional tariffs on Chinese-origin goods (currently 25–145% on many categories). If your products are manufactured in mainland China but exported from Taiwan, CBP scrutinizes the origin claim. Products that are "substantially transformed" in Taiwan qualify for Taiwan origin and avoid Section 301. If you're re-exporting Chinese goods via Taiwan without value-added manufacturing, this is customs fraud risk.

**FCC**: Any electronic device with a wireless component (Bluetooth, WiFi, cellular) sold in the US needs FCC certification. No exceptions. Certification cost: $3,000–15,000 per device, 6–12 weeks.

### Japan

**Regulatory body**: Japan Customs + Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (MHLW)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Cosmetics | Quasi-drug/cosmetic ingredient list must comply with MHLW positive list |
| Food | Food Sanitation Act compliance; ingredients must be permitted |
| Electronics | PSE mark (electrical safety) + Giteki mark (radio equipment) |
| All imports | Consumption tax (10%) applies above JPY 10,000 |

**Duty rates** (Japan-Taiwan have a quasi-FTA via ASTEP, but not full FTA):
- Electronics: 0% on most (Japan has low electronics duties)
- Apparel: 8–10%
- Cosmetics: Free–6.6%
- Food: Varies widely; agricultural products can be 50%+

**Quasi-drug designation**: Some products sold as cosmetics in Taiwan (e.g., whitening creams, anti-dandruff shampoos) are classified as "quasi-drugs" (医薬部外品) in Japan and require pre-market approval. This is a common trap for Taiwan beauty brands entering Japan.

**Japanese labeling requirement**: All products sold commercially in Japan must have Japanese-language labeling (品名, 内容量, 製造者/輸入者名住所). This applies to the product itself, not just the outer shipping box.

### South Korea

**Regulatory body**: Korea Customs Service (KCS) + MSIT (electronics) + MFDS (food/cosmetics)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Electronics | KC certification (Korea Certification) mandatory for most electronics |
| Cosmetics | MFDS import notification + Korean-language labeling |
| Food | MFDS import declaration + Korean-language labeling |
| All imports >USD 150 | Formal customs declaration + VAT (10%) |

**KC Certification trap**: Similar to FCC in the US, KC certification is mandatory for electronics. Without it, products are seized at customs. Certification: $2,000–8,000 per product, 4–8 weeks.

**Korea–Taiwan**: No bilateral FTA. MFN tariff rates apply. Korea does have FTAs with the US, EU, ASEAN — Taiwan is at a competitive disadvantage for some categories.

---

### Australia

**Regulatory body**: Australian Border Force (ABF) + TGA (therapeutic goods) + DAFF (food/agriculture)

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| All imports | GST (10%) collected by overseas vendor if revenue >AUD 75,000/year |
| Therapeutic goods (supplements, medical devices) | TGA registration — extensive and expensive |
| Food | Biosecurity Act compliance; certain foods prohibited or require inspection |
| Electronics | RCM mark (Regulatory Compliance Mark) for electrical safety |
| Wood products | Biosecurity declaration |

**GST registration requirement**: Since July 2018, overseas vendors with >AUD 75,000 annual revenue from Australian customers must register for GST and collect 10% at checkout. Major marketplaces handle this automatically. For DTC sellers, check your revenue against this threshold.

**Therapeutic Goods Act**: Australia is one of the strictest markets for supplements, vitamins, and medical devices. Products that would be sold as general food in Taiwan may be classified as therapeutic goods in Australia and require TGA registration ($5,000–50,000+ per product).

## HS Code Selection

The Harmonized System (HS) code determines:
1. Import duty rate
2. Whether the product needs permits/licenses
3. Whether quotas apply
4. Statistical tracking

**6-digit international HS = shared globally. 8-10 digits = country-specific.**

### Common Taiwan Export Categories

| Product Type | HS Chapter | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| Consumer electronics | 84, 85 | Chapter 85 for most — specify by function |
| Cosmetics/skincare | 3304 | Sub-code matters: lipstick vs. skincare vs. hair care |
| Food supplements | 2106 | Or 3004 if classified as pharma — large duty difference |
| Apparel | 61 (knit), 62 (woven) | Fiber content determines sub-code |
| Plastic housewares | 3924 | |
| Toys | 9503 | |
| Jewelry (fashion) | 7117 | Fine jewelry: 7113–7116 |
| Bags/handbags | 4202 | Material determines sub-code |

### How to Verify HS Code

1. **Taiwan source**: [財政部關務署 Web Tariff](https://tariff.customs.gov.tw/) — Taiwan's Customs tariff schedule. Use this to find your product's Taiwan export HS code.
2. **Destination verification**: Each country publishes its own tariff schedule. Cross-check using:
   - Malaysia: [MyATIS](https://www.customs.gov.my/en/)
   - US: [USITC DataWeb](https://dataweb.usitc.gov/) / HTS Online
   - Japan: [Japan Customs Tariff Schedule](https://www.customs.go.jp/english/tariff/)
   - Korea: [KCS Tariff Schedule](https://www.customs.go.kr/english/)
3. **Confirm with your freight forwarder**: For first shipments to a new market, pay for a formal HS binding ruling or ask your 3PL's customs broker to verify. Wrong HS codes attract penalties in every market.

**Misclassification penalty example (US)**: CBP can assess 4x the unpaid duty on misdeclared merchandise, plus potential seizure. For a $10,000 shipment misclassified to avoid $500 in duty, the penalty can be $2,000.

## Required Documents — Master Checklist

| Document | When Required | Content |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| Commercial Invoice | Always | Seller/buyer info, HS code, declared value (CIF or FOB), incoterms, country of origin |
| Packing List | Always | Item count, weights, dimensions per carton |
| Airway Bill / Bill of Lading | Always | Shipping document from courier/freight |
| Certificate of Origin (CO) | When claiming FTA rates or required by destination | Issued by Bureau of Foreign Trade (Taiwan) or TAITRA |
| Material Safety Data Sheet | Chemicals, batteries, hazardous materials | Required by airline/courier regardless of customs |
| Phytosanitary Certificate | Wood products, plants | Issued by BAPHIQ (Taiwan) |
| Health Certificate | Food exports | Issued by relevant Taiwan authority |
| Product-specific permits | Electronics, cosmetics, food, medical devices | Country-specific — see above |

**Invoice declared value**: Always declare the actual transaction value. Under-declaration (under-invoicing) to reduce duty is customs fraud. Penalties include seizure, fines, and blacklisting by courier. Many countries now compare declared values against market prices algorithmically.

## Country Selection Matrix

Before entering a new market, score it against these customs-specific factors:

| Factor | Low Friction | High Friction |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| De minimis threshold | >USD 100 | <USD 50 |
| Product certification required | No/simple | Yes (SIRIM, KC, FCC, etc.) |
| Import permits for category | Not required | Required (BPOM, TGA, FDA) |
| FTA with Taiwan | Yes | No (most markets) |
| Duty rate for your HS code | <5% | >15% |
| Customs enforcement predictability | High (SG, JP, AU) | Low (VN, ID informal) |

**Recommended entry sequence for Taiwan sellers** based on customs complexity (easiest first):
1. Singapore — English, transparent, low duties, established logistics
2. USA — High de minimis ($800) removes most friction for sub-$800 items
3. Japan — Predictable, low duties on most electronics, strict on cosmetics/food
4. Malaysia — Moderate complexity, large Mandarin-speaking market
5. Philippines — Lower barrier than Indonesia, growing middle class
6. Australia — GST registration needed above AUD 75K revenue
7. Thailand — Low de minimis, strict FDA for cosmetics/food
8. South Korea — KC certification required for electronics
9. Indonesia — BPOM for cosmetics/food is a major barrier; high tariffs

## Recurring Compliance Calendar

Once operational in a market, track these recurring obligations:

| Market | Recurring Task | Frequency |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| Singapore | GST filing (if registered) | Quarterly |
| Australia | GST BAS filing (if registered) | Quarterly |
| USA | FCC renewal | Not required (one-time, but tied to product model) |
| Malaysia | SIRIM renewal | Per product lifecycle change |
| Japan | Consumption tax filing | Annual (if registered non-resident) |
| Korea | KC renewal | Not required unless product changes |
| Indonesia | BPOM renewal | Every 2–5 years depending on category |
