# Financial Journalism Ethics & Legal Red Lines

Taiwan financial reporters operate under specific statutory frameworks. This file covers securities law, defamation, analyst conflicts, and timing-based risks.

---

## Securities Law & Market Manipulation

### 證交法 §155 — Market Manipulation Prohibition

**The law:**
- Prohibits spreading false rumors or creating false market impressions to manipulate stock price
- Applies to anyone: company officers, journalists, bloggers

**Journalism risk:**
- Publishing unverified negative claims about a company's finances can trigger §155 liability
- "Broadcasting falsehoods" (傳播不實資訊) is the charge; truth is a defense, but you must be able to prove it

**Practice:**
- ❌ "Company A is insolvent" (unless you can cite audited financials proving insolvency)
- ✅ "Company A's CFO declined to comment on the rumor; audited financials will be released next week"
- ✅ "Whistleblower alleges [specific claim]; company denies it; investigation pending"

**With analyst quotes:**
- If quoting an analyst saying stock is overvalued, disclose analyst's firm, rating, and any holdings
- Analysts have stake in market movements (firm's trading desk, brokerage commissions); readers need to know

---

### 證交法 §157-1 — Insider Trading Prohibition

**The law:**
- Prohibits trading on material non-public information
- "Material" = information that could affect stock price or investor decisions

**Journalism risk:**
- Publishing information before 公開資訊觀測站 official disclosure can expose reporter to insider-trading accusations
- If you get a tip on earnings before release, do not publish until official announcement

**Practice:**
- Always cite 公開資訊觀測站 timestamp, not company press-release time
- If story breaks from your own sources (interview, document), verify through official channels before publishing
- Embargo periods: some companies embargo earnings info until official release time; honor embargoes

**Red flag scenario:**
- CFO tells you (off-record) about earnings before public announcement
- You write it up but don't publish until official release
- Still risky: if questioned, can you prove your source didn't profit from advance knowledge?
- Safer: stick to official sources, or mark information as "unverified pending official release"

## Defamation & Reputation

### 刑法 §310 — Criminal Defamation (Taiwan)

**The law:**
- Publishing statements that damage someone's reputation is punishable
- Truth is a defense (但須能舉證證明為真實)
- Public-interest defense exists but is narrow: must have public benefit and reasonable belief in truth

**Journalism risk:**
- Negative claims about executives (incompetence, fraud, misconduct) can trigger defamation suits
- "Company X is run by fraudsters" (without evidence) = high risk

**Practice for earnings reporting:**
- ❌ "CEO is hiding losses to inflate stock price" (accusation without evidence)
- ✅ "Whistleblower claims CEO hid losses; CEO denies; SEC investigating; we will update as investigation progresses"
- ✅ "Auditor qualified its opinion on accounts receivable valuation; company explains [CFO statement]"

**Safe language:**
- Use reporting verbs: "alleged", "claimed", "according to", "SEC filing states"
- Not: "Company fraudulently..."

## Analyst Disclosure & Conflicts

### Analyst Stake Disclosure

**Good practice (varies by jurisdiction; Taiwan follows general principles):**
- When quoting analyst's buy/sell rating, state:
  1. Analyst's firm name
  2. Rating (buy/hold/sell)
  3. Any corporate relationship (if analyst's firm is underwriter, advisor, or debt issuer)
  4. Any stock holdings (if firm owns target company's stock)

**Examples:**

- ❌ "Analyst Lee says stock is undervalued"
- ✅ "Lee Chen, equity analyst at Yuanta Securities (buy-rated, $420 price target), says stock is undervalued. Yuanta does not hold stock in Company X; the firm was company's debt adviser in 2022."
- ✅ "Analyst Wong (neutral-rated, $380 target) at Nomura says growth story is mature. Nomura makes markets in Company X's bonds and discussed investment-banking opportunities in 2025."

**Why it matters:**
- Analyst working for company's long-time underwriter may have institutional bias toward positivity
- Analyst whose firm makes markets in company's debt has interest in company staying solvent (may downplay risk)
- Analyst whose firm is bidding for M&A business is conflicted (incentive to trumpet deal benefits)

## Timing-Sensitive Disclosures

### 公開資訊觀測站 Timestamp vs. Press Release

**The risk:**
- Company announces earnings in press release at 5pm
- MOPS filing comes at 6pm (after market close)
- If you publish at 5:15pm using press release only, you may have published before official disclosure

**Practice:**
- Always cite 公開資訊觀測站 timestamp as the official "when the market knew"
- Note any time gap: "Company released statement at 5pm; MOPS filing at 6pm"
- If gap is material (e.g., after-hours news affecting next trading day), warn readers that trading did not occur on this news

---

### Embargo Periods & Private Information

**Company embargo practice:**
- Some companies release earnings to journalists under embargo (e.g., "Hold for 8am release")
- This is contractual; breaking embargo can expose reporter to legal action

**Practice:**
- Honor embargoes as agreed
- If you cannot abide by embargo, inform company upfront and do not accept the document

## Undisclosed Conflicts in Your Own Newsroom

### AI-Generated or AI-Assisted Content Disclosure

- If this skill or any AI tool significantly contributed to financial analysis in the piece, disclose it
- Example footnote: "This story benefited from AI-assisted data processing and fact-checking; all figures were independently verified by the author."

### Affiliate Interests

- If your news organization benefits financially from the stock you cover (affiliate links, sponsored content), disclose it
- Taiwan journalism ethics codes require disclosure of such relationships

## Guidance for Sensitive Sub-Types

### Fraud/Bankruptcy Allegations

- Source: Usually whistleblower, competitor, or regulatory filing
- **Must verify** against company response, auditor opinion, or official investigation status
- **Mark language:** "alleges", "claims", "according to filing"
- **Give company fair chance to respond** before publishing
- **Disclose source motivation** if relevant: competitor = commercial interest, disgruntled employee = personal grievance

### M&A / Takeover Rumors

- Source: Often "sources close to" deal team
- **Requires corroboration** from 2+ independent sources or official regulatory filing
- **Market-moving**: rumors alone can trigger trading halts
- **Safe language:** "Sources close to deal discussions say [X]; companies have not confirmed"

### Executive Changes / Leadership Succession

- Speculation on CEO replacement has reputational/market impact
- **Only publish if:**
  1. Company official confirms, OR
  2. Multiple credible sources, OR
  3. Official board announcement / regulatory filing
- **Never publish speculation as fact**

## Cross-Border Considerations

### US Securities Reporting

If covering US-listed company or US parent of Taiwan subsidiary:

- **SEC Rule 10b-5** (anti-fraud): similar to Taiwan §155/§157-1
- **Reg FD** (Fair Disclosure): public companies must disclose material info broadly (no selective briefings to journalists)
- **Safe harbor** for forward-looking statements (guidance) — but must include risk factors

### Hong Kong / Greater China

- **Hong Kong Securities Law**: similar insider-trading & market-manipulation rules
- **Mainland PRC**: Different legal framework; exercise high caution with claims about mainland firms

## Checklist Before Publishing Financial News

- [ ] **Numbers traced to source:** All figures cite 公開資訊觀測站, SEC EDGAR, or audited filing
- [ ] **One-time items disclosed:** Any one-time gains/losses separated from operating income
- [ ] **Analyst conflicts disclosed:** If quoting analyst, state firm, rating, corporate relationships
- [ ] **Company response given:** If story includes accusations, company was contacted and response (or lack thereof) noted
- [ ] **No insider knowledge published early:** All figures from official disclosure, not pre-release tips
- [ ] **Currency/scope explicit:** Every number has (fiscal year), (consolidated/individual), (currency if non-NT$)
- [ ] **Valuation claims have benchmarks:** P/E, ROE, multiples cited vs. peer or historical average
- [ ] **Language is precise:** Use "alleged", "estimated", "projected" where appropriate; avoid "clearly", "obviously", "frankly"
- [ ] **Defamation risk checked:** No unsubstantiated personal accusations about executives
- [ ] **AI disclosure (if applicable):** If AI tool materially contributed, disclose in footnote
