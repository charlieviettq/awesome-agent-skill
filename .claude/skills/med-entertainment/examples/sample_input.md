Demo Night Screening — Personal Notes (Embargoed until 2026-05-02 14:00)
Screened: 2026-04-28 at Demo Pictures advance theatre

Immediate reaction: The film earns its long takes. What could have felt self-indulgent 
instead becomes meditative. Maggie's (林小華) closing monologue in the final 10 minutes 
— shot in one continuous take, no cuts — broke the silence of the theatre.

Issues: 
- The second act drags slightly. A 2:15 runtime at times feels like 2:45.
- The father (Sato Taro) has minimal screen time; his character remains opaque.
- Some dialogue feels on-the-nose for a film that otherwise prizes subtlety.

Strengths:
- The sound design. Yamamoto's use of silence and ambient noise is as precise as the visuals.
- The three lead performances are genuine; no false notes.
- Visually coherent across 140 locations — remarkable production design.

Comparison: If Hirokazu Koreeda made a Taiwan family drama with 30% less sentimentality, 
this might be the result.

Box office potential: Family drama in Taiwan cinemas has a modest floor (~NTD 20-30M opening 
weekend for a quality indie film). This has A-list production values and cultural relevance; 
could reach NTD 40-60M if word-of-mouth is strong. Overseas festival circuit will matter.
