# Entertainment News Sources & Beats

## Taiwan-Specific Entertainment News Sources

### Government & Public Bodies
- **文化部影視及流行音樂產業局** (TAICCA — Taiwan Creative Content Agency)
  - Subsidies, grants, film fund allocations, policy announcements
  - Official film & streaming market data, copyright / talent protections
  - Source tier: Official public record (Tier 1)
  - URL: https://taicca.tw/

- **國家電影中心** (Taiwan National Film Center)
  - Box office data, Taiwan theatrical release records, archival information
  - Tier: Public record (Tier 1)
  - Note: May take 1-2 weeks lag on grosses

- **台灣電影中心** (Taiwan Film Institute)
  - Film research, archival, heritage protection
  - Tier: Official source for historical context

### Industry Associations & Press Organizations
- **台灣電影製作協會** (Taiwan Film Producers Association)
  - Industry data, production budgets (sometimes), member news
  - Tier: Industry official (Tier 2 — verify key facts independently)

- **台灣電視學會** (Taiwan Broadcasting & Television Association)
  - TV production trends, OTT policy

### Major Studios & Production Houses (Taiwan & Regional)
- **華視**, **民視**, **三立**, **TVBS** — traditional broadcast sources, proprietary shows
- **Netflix Taiwan**, **Disney+ Taiwan**, **Apple TV+ Taiwan**, **Amazon Prime Video Taiwan** — streaming platforms (self-reported data, Tier 3)
- Independent production studios (publish press releases; Tier 2-3 depending on outlet)

### Independent Entertainment Media & Critics
- **放映週報** (Funscreen Weekly) — serious film criticism, Taiwan/international
  - Tier: Independent critic (Tier 2)

- **電影欣賞** (Film Appreciation Magazine)
  - Academic film criticism

- **KINO News** — international film news (with Taiwan focus)
  - Tier: Aggregator with analysis

### International Entertainment Media (reference / context)
- **Variety** — entertainment industry standard, A-tier source
  - Beats: Hollywood, casting, box office, festival news
  - Tier: Industry-standard journalism (Tier 2)
  - Limitation: US-centric; Taiwan coverage sporadic

- **Hollywood Reporter** — box office, production news, deal tracking
  - Tier: Industry journalism (Tier 2)

- **Deadline** — entertainment deal news, production announcements
  - Tier: Industry journalism (Tier 2)

- **The Wrap** — entertainment criticism & industry analysis
  - Tier: Journalist-aggregator (Tier 2-3)

- **IndieWire** — independent & arthouse film focus
  - Tier: Specialist journalism (Tier 2)

### Streaming Platform Data Sources (Self-Reported)
- **Netflix Top 10 / Top 10 Global** — watch hours (Netflix's metric)
  - Limitation: Platform-proprietary, not independent verification
  - Tier: Self-reported platform data (Tier 3)
  - Cross-platform comparison: **NOT VALID** (different metrics)

- **Disney+ viewership claims** — accounts engaged (Disney's metric)
  - Limitation: Infrequently published; estimates from financial reports
  - Tier: Self-reported (Tier 3)

- **Apple TV+ press statements** — minimal public disclosure
  - Limitation: Very selective disclosure
  - Tier: Self-reported (Tier 3)

### Box Office & Data Aggregators
- **Box Office Mojo** — US/global theatrical box office (authoritative for Western releases)
  - Tier: Industry-standard data source (Tier 1 for US; less reliable for Taiwan)
  - Note: Taiwan data often incomplete; National Film Center is authoritative for Taiwan

- **IMDb** — film metadata, ratings aggregation
  - Limitation: User-generated ratings subject to brigading
  - Tier: Public record metadata (Tier 1 for facts), aggregated user rating (Tier 3, use with caution)

- **Rotten Tomatoes** — critic aggregation (Tomatometer) vs audience ratings (Audience Score)
  - Limitation: Audience score subject to review-bombing
  - Critic score: Tier 2 (journalist consensus)
  - Audience score: Tier 3 (user-generated, verify context for controversies)

- **Metacritic** — critic-aggregated review scores
  - Tier: Journalist consensus (Tier 2)

### Festival & Awards Information
- **台灣國際紀錄片雙年展** (Taiwan International Documentary Film Festival)
- **台北電影節** (Taipei Film Festival)
- **台灣國際女性影展** (Taiwan International Women Film Festival)
- **金馬獎** (Golden Horse Awards) — Asia's Oscar, Taiwan-based
  - Tier: Official award body (Tier 1 for selections/winners)

- **cannes.com** / **berlin.de** / **tiff.net** — major international festivals
  - Tier: Official (Tier 1 for selections)

### Entertainment-News Beats (Taiwan-Specific)

| Beat | Primary Sources | Secondary Sources | Verification |
|------|---|---|---|
| **New film releases / premieres** | Studio press releases, TAICCA announcements, National Film Center | Variety, trade publications | Verify release date against official distributor; embargo dates |
| **Box office** | National Film Center, Box Office Mojo | Industry analysts | Check methodology (Taiwan vs global, opening weekend vs cumulative) |
| **Streaming platform news** | Platform press releases, financial reports | Industry observers (Deadline, Variety) | Platform self-reports; cross-verify with third-party analysts if available |
| **Casting / talent announcements** | Talent agency press releases, entertainment news sites | IMDb, filmography records | Verify title and production stage (announced vs greenlit vs filming) |
| **International festival selections** | Official festival websites | Entertainment media coverage | Go to official festival site; do not rely solely on press release framing |
| **Industry policy / regulation** | TAICCA, Ministry of Culture, legislature | Legal specialists | Confirm statutory language; do not paraphrase policy without official text |
| **Film criticism / reviews** | Independent critics, filmmaker interviews | Aggregators (RT, Metacritic) | Conduct independent screening or document embargo date; do not summarize junket materials |
| **Entertainment industry trends** | TAICCA reports, academic research | Variety, trade analysts | Distinguish between data (e.g., production volume) vs. interpretation (e.g., "golden age") |

## Common Pitfalls in Entertainment Source Vetting

- **Studio press kit ≠ news**: Rephrasing press-kit language without disclosure is PR amplification, not reporting.
- **Festival selection ≠ endorsement**: A film's selection at Cannes is a fact; calling it a "triumph" or "discovery" is editorial framing.
- **Aggregate scores ≠ critical consensus**: A Rotten Tomatoes Tomatometer of 78% means ~78% of critics gave thumbs-up; it does not mean the film is "widely praised" (which is editorial framing).
- **Streaming "Top 10" ≠ success metric**: Top 10 on Netflix Taiwan reflects Netflix's proprietary watch-hour metric, which cannot be compared to Disney+ (accounts engaged) or theatrical (ticket sales).
- **Talent agency press release ≠ verified fact**: "An A-list actor is set to star in a film" ≠ the actor is confirmed/finalized. Check production status (option / negotiation / confirmed) before reporting.
