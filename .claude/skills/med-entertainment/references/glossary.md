# Entertainment & Film Industry Glossary

## Box Office & Revenue Terminology

### Box Office Measurement
- **Domestic gross**: Revenue from theatrical releases in the film's "home" country (e.g., Taiwan release = Taiwan box office).
- **International gross / Overseas gross**: Revenue from all non-domestic territories combined.
- **Worldwide / Global gross**: Domestic + international combined.
- **Opening weekend**: Typically Friday–Sunday (3 days in some markets, 4 days in others). Taiwan convention: Friday–Sunday. US: Friday–Sunday.
- **Cumulative gross / Total gross**: Final lifetime box office after theatrical run ends.
- **Platform release** (limited): theatrical release in <500 theatres, typically for indie / arthouse films. Compare: wide release (1000+ theatres, major studio films).

### Box Office Calculations & Caveats
- **Net vs. Gross**: Reported box office is *gross* (total tickets), not *net* (studio's take after theatre cut, typically 50–55%). Context: a NTD 100M gross might yield NTD 45–50M net to studio.
- **Same-week comparisons are invalid across years**: 「2026 opening weekend第一名」vs 「2025 highest weekend」are not apples-to-apples if released in different seasons or market sizes.
- **Adjusted for inflation**: Old films' box office numbers should be inflation-adjusted for fair comparison. Rarely done in entertainment media; always note when you do.

## Streaming & Digital Metrics

### Streaming Measurement (Platform-Specific)
- **Netflix**: Watch Hours — total hours of content watched in a territory/period. NOT "viewers" or "accounts". 
  - Implication: a film watched by 10 people for 2 hours = 20 watch hours, not 10 accounts.
  - Cross-platform comparison: **INVALID** (Netflix does not publish accounts; Disney+ uses accounts; Apple TV+ uses neither).

- **Disney+**: Accounts Engaged — number of Disney+ accounts that watched at least 2 minutes of the content.
  - Implication: 1 account = 1 person (usually); 5-person household watching together = 1 account.
  - Cross-platform comparison: **INVALID** (different metric from Netflix/Apple).

- **Apple TV+**: Minimal public disclosure. Few metrics available. Sometimes estimated from financial reports, but not official.

- **YouTube / YouTube Premium**: Views (play count) + watch time. Highly transparent but not comparable to Netflix/Disney+.

### Interpretation Red Flags
- "X is #1 on Netflix" = among Netflix content in that territory. Does NOT imply highest-grossing or highest-quality; merely highest watch hours that week.
- "Netflix released X" / "Disney+ original" = platform-exclusive or platform-premiered. Does not imply Netflix produced it (may be licensed third-party content).
- "Top 10" turnover: Netflix top 10 can shift daily or weekly; always note the date of your citation. A film in top 10 one week may be #37 the next.

## Film & Entertainment Production Terminology

### Production Phases
- **Development / Greenlit**: Project is in early stages, funding approved in principle.
- **Pre-production**: Crew hired, script finalized, locations scouted. No filming yet.
- **Principal photography / Filming**: Active shooting underway. Typically 40–120 days depending on scale.
- **Post-production / Editing**: Filming complete; editing, sound design, color grading, visual effects in progress.
- **Locked / Locked cut**: Final edit approved; no further changes. Colour and sound mixing in progress.
- **DCP / Final delivery**: Digital Cinema Package prepared for theatrical distribution; final format completed.

### Production Roles & Hierarchy
- **Producer / Executive Producer**: Finance, hiring, creative oversight (varies by contract; can range from crucial to ceremonial).
- **Director of Photography (DP) / Cinematographer**: Visual look; camera, lighting, composition.
- **Production Designer / Art Director**: Set design, costumes, visual world.
- **Editor / Film Editor**: Cuts footage into final shape; pacing, rhythm, narrative structure.
- **Sound Designer**: Sound effects, ambient sound design (distinct from composer / music).
- **Composer**: Original music score.
- **Line Producer**: Budget & logistics; distinct from creative producer.

### Financial Terminology
- **Production budget**: Cost to film (crew, equipment, locations, cast). Typical range: indie NTD 30–50M, mid-budget NTD 80–200M, blockbuster NTD 300M+.
- **Below-the-line budget**: Crew, equipment, logistics costs (as opposed to above-the-line: writer, director, talent).
- **Marketing budget**: Advertising, promotion, press junkets, festival participation. Often 50% of production budget or higher for wide releases.
- **Break-even**: When cumulative revenue (theatrical + streaming + licensing) exceeds total production + marketing spend.

## Talent & Industry Representation

### Talent Representation
- **Agent**: Represents actor/director for deal negotiation, contract terms. Typically 10% commission.
- **Manager**: Longer-term career guidance; may also negotiate (sometimes overlap with agent role).
- **Publicist**: Handles press, interviews, social media, public image.
- **Legal representative / Entertainment lawyer**: Contracts, rights, IP issues.

### Contract Terms (Simplified)
- **Pay-or-play**: Talent is paid regardless of whether the production moves forward.
- **Approval rights**: Talent may have script approval, director approval, co-star approval, or credit approval.
- **First-look deal**: Studio has first opportunity to finance a producer/director's next project.
- **Back-end / Participation**: Talent receives percentage of gross (rare) or net (common) revenue post-breakeven.

## Film Evaluation & Aggregation

### Rating / Scoring Systems
- **Rotten Tomatoes Tomatometer**: Aggregate of critic reviews; % represents critics who gave positive reviews (not average star rating).
  - 75%+ = "Fresh" (generally favorable)
  - <60% = "Rotten" (generally unfavorable)
  - **Limitation**: Does not measure degree of favorability; 75% could mean "lukewarm thumbs-up from everyone" or "50% loved it, 50% hated it".

- **Rotten Tomatoes Audience Score**: User-submitted ratings aggregated. Subject to review-bombing by coordinated fans or detractors.
  - Higher variance than Tomatometer; may differ by 10–30 points in controversial films.
  - Note if there's a significant gap (Critic: 78%, Audience: 43% = likely brigading or fundamental disagreement on merit).

- **IMDb Rating**: User-submitted ratings (1–10 stars) averaged. More stable than RT Audience Score but still vulnerable to manipulation.
  - Weighted algorithm attempts to reduce spam; still subject to shift if a fandom mobilizes.
  - Cite the rating + number of ratings to provide context. (E.g., "7.8/10 from 120,000 ratings" vs. "7.8/10 from 8,000 ratings" are different confidence levels.)

- **Metacritic Score**: Similar to RT Tomatometer but uses weighted averaging of professional critics. Scale: 0–100.
  - 80+ = "Universal Acclaim"
  - 60–79 = "Mixed or Average"
  - <60 = "Generally Unfavorable"

### Film Festival Terminology
- **Premiere**: First public screening. "World premiere" = first anywhere; "North American premiere" = first in that region.
- **Competition / In Competition**: Film officially entered in festival's main award category.
- **Official Selection / Out of Competition**: Film selected by festival but not in running for top prize (sometimes indicates conflict of interest, sometimes just scheduling).
- **Jury Prize / Special Mention**: Award categories; lower-tier than main prize.

## Distribution & Release Terminology

### Release Strategy
- **Theatrical release / Festival run**: Traditional cinema screening.
- **Streaming premiere / Direct-to-streaming**: Bypasses theatrical; releases on platform (Netflix, Disney+, etc.).
- **Simultaneous release / Same-day release**: Theatrical + streaming on same date. Became common post-2020.
- **Windowing**: Staggered release (theatrical → rental → purchase → subscription). Typical window: 45 days theatrical exclusivity before VOD (video-on-demand); 6+ months before subscription platforms.
- **VOD / Video-on-demand**: Rental or purchase (iTunes, Google Play, etc.). Consumer pays per viewing or for rental period (usually 48 hours).

### Territory & Localization
- **Dubbing**: Spoken dialogue replaced in target language (common in Asia, less common in English-speaking markets).
- **Subtitling**: Original language dialogue + translated text; audience reads subtitles.
- **Localized title**: Title changed for target market for cultural relevance (E.g., 「示範之夜」might be "Demo Night" internationally, but "Mother's Return" in certain markets for recognition).

## Entertainment Industry Jargon & Slang

- **Greenlight**: Formal approval to proceed with production.
- **Turnaround**: Project shelved / rights returned to producer after studio rejects it.
- **Back nine**: Television series shortened from initial 22-episode order to 9 episodes (cost-cutting).
- **Notes**: Feedback from producer / network / studio on script or cut.
- **Junket / Press junket**: Studio-organized press tour for cast/crew; includes interviews, photo calls, panel discussions.
- **Embargo**: Contractual prohibition on publishing until a specified date/time.
- **Wire / Wired**: Publication of interview or feature is imminent; "going live" soon.

## Legal & Ethical Terminology

- **Work-for-hire**: Creator is paid a flat fee; company owns all copyright (standard for screenwriters, directors on studio projects).
- **Credit / On-screen credit**: How a person's name appears in film (director, producer, editor, etc.). Disputes over credit are common.
- **Residuals / Reuse fees**: Talent receives additional payment when their work is re-broadcast, licensed, or re-released.
- **Right of publicity / Publicity rights**: A person's right to control use of their likeness, name, voice.
- **Defamation / Libel**: False factual statement that damages reputation (applies to film plots / reviews if they assert false facts about named real people).
- **Spoiler**: Plot detail that reveals surprise / ending; ethical journalism spoiler warning is standard practice.
